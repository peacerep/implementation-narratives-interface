FACTS MATTER  Between 23 August and 5 September, the SMM recorded a decrease in the number of ceasefire violations – 2,494 compared with 2,966 in the previous two-week period.

 The Mission continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, recording 53 ceasefire violations inside the area near Petrivske.

 The SMM corroborated reports of five civilian casualties, including a fatality, bringing the total number of confirmed civilian casualties since the beginning of 2021 to 62 (15 fatalities and 47 injuries).

Thirty-six of these casualties were due to mines, unexploded ordnance and other explosive objects, nine due to small-arms fire and 17 due to shelling.

 The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 30 times, including 27 instances in non-government-controlled areas.

SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) were as- sessed as targeted by small-arms fire on 12 occasions:

two times in government- controlled and ten times in non-government-controlled areas.

The Mission’s UAVs experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming on 107 occa- sions, while flying on both sides of the contact line.

 On 31 August, a SMM patrol of five Mission members heard projectiles passing above their heads and impact explosions nearby in non-government-controlled Oleksandriv- ka, Donetsk region.

The patrol safely left the area (see SMM Daily Report of 1 Septem- ber 2021).

 The Mission continued to observe civilians while crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line and the challenges they face.

The SMM again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska, open daily, and Novo- troitske/Olenivka, open twice a week, as the corresponding checkpoint of the armed formations is open for crossing only on Mondays and Fridays.

 The SMM continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to ena- ble the maintenance and operation of critical civilian infrastructure, including repairs to the phenol sludge reservoir near Nelipivka, as well as to power lines near Nevelske, Novoluhanske and in Krasnohorivka, all in Donetsk region.

Since the beginning of the year, the Mission has facilitated and monitored adherence to localized ceasefires to enable repairs and maintenance of 75 public infrastructure sites on both sides of the contact line (43 in Donetsk region and 32 in Luhansk region), providing water, electrici- ty, gas and mobile communications to about five million civilians.
