Between 17 and 30 September, the SMM recorded almost 9,000 ceasefire violations, compared with over 10,000 in the previous two weeks. The violence continues despite recommitments since the beginning of the year. The last recommitment took place on 29 August. three attempts

The SMM continued to observe weapons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines (78 over the two-week period (42 in areas not controlled by the Government)).

Since the beginning of the year, the SMM has corroborated 200 civilian casualties (37 deaths and 163 injuries), including 28 children. On 30 September, three children were killed and two were injured in two separate incidents near Horlivka and Staromykhailivka.

The Mission facilitated and monitored adherence to the ceasefire to enable repairs and maintenance to critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line. Of note, it mounted over 40 patrols to oversee ‘windows of silence’ near Artema and Yasynuvata to ensure continued water supply to more than 1.3 million people. In his address to the OSCE Permanent Council in Vienna on 27 September, OSCE SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan stressed the need for political will to translate commitments already made into concrete action and to find more long-lasting solutions.
