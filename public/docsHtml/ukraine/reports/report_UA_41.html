LATEST NEWS 

The SMM observed an overall decrease in ceasefire violations across Donetsk region during 25-31 July, following an increase the previous week.

 The hotspots remain the Yasynuvata-Avdiivka-Donetsk airport triangle, areas around Horlivka, the Svitlodarsk-Debaltseve road, and areas east and north- east of Mariupol.

In Luhansk region, the SMM observed high levels of violence in western parts of the region, and around Shchastia and Stanytsia Luhanska.

 The SMM monitors and reports around-the-clock with around 90 ground patrols a day and night-time monitoring conducted from 14 locations in Donbas, and by using static 24/7 cameras and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) and other aerial surveillance.

Watch examples of night-time footage filmed by SMM UAVs at http://bit.ly/2aKsUzP  The SMM continued to observe the presence and use of proscribed weapons, including multiple-launch rocket systems, and numerous weapons missing from storage sites.

 The sides continued to impose restrictions on the SMM’s freedom of movement, the majority of which occurred in areas outside of government control.

On 25 July an SMM long-range UAV crashed in Donetsk region, a mini UAV came under fire in Luhansk region (see Spot report:

www.osce.org/ ukraine-smm/256371).

 On 28 July Ertugrul Apakan, the Chief Monitor of the SMM addressed the OSCE Permanent Council in Vienna, and called for urgent steps by the sides to ensure full respect for the ceasefire and reduce the impact of the violence on civilians.
