LATEST NEWS 

The OSCE Special Monitoring Mission to Ukraine (SMM) observed a significant increase in the number of ceasefire violations in both Donetsk and Luhansk regions.

Most violence occurred around Donetsk airport;

government-controlled Avdiivka, “DPR”-controlled Yasynuvata;

on the road between “DPR”-controlled Debaltseve and government-controlled Svitlodarsk;

and in the area around the bridge south of government-controlled Stanytsia Luhanska.



The SMM continued to note a tense situation in the Berezove-Dokuchaievsk area, approximately 30km south-west of Donetsk.

Eight thousand people in the area – on both sides of the contact line – are without potable water since 15 April.

Near government-controlled Berezove, repair work required to fix electricity lines downed by shelling is impossible because the area is heavily mined.



The SMM observed numerous weapons missing from storage sites and the presence of weapons in violation of withdrawal lines.



Freedom of movement of SMM monitors continued to be restricted, particularly in areas not controlled by the government.



On 27 May SMM monitors were fired upon (See spot report:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/243366).

In the reporting period two SMM long-range unmanned aerial vehicles were lost over “DPR”-controlled areas (see Spot Reports:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/244576 and www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/243361 ).


