The security situation in eastern Ukraine during the last two weeks remained tense with the SMM recording 11,425 ceasefire violations in total along the contact line. The Mission noted escalation in a number of areas, notably in the Avdiivka- Yasynuvata-Donetsk airport area from 30 October to 5 November and in areas east and north-east of Mariupol from 6 to 12 November.

Factors contributing to such violence – namely the sides’ refusal to fully cease fire, withdraw heavy weapons, disengage forces and formations, and de-mine – were evident throughout the reporting period. The Mission observed 310 heavy weapons that should have been withdrawn according to the Minsk agreements; an increased number of ceasefire violations within the Petrivske disengagement area towards the end of the reporting period; and mines and unexploded ordnance in many areas, including in and around entry-exit checkpoints used by civilians.

The plight of the civilian population in eastern Ukraine has been exacerbated as winter sets in, with many areas without electricity, water and gas supply. The Mission over the reporting period monitored and facilitated 42 “windows of silence” allowing for inspection, repair and maintenance work to 12 infrastructure facilities on both sides of the contact line. For example, on 10 November the SMM continued to facilitate and monitor repairs and maintenance work, co-ordinated by the Joint Centre for Control and Co-ordination (JCCC), to the power plant in Shchastia, high-voltage lines between Troitske and Novozvanivka, power lines between Dovhe and Slovianoserbsk, water wells near Stanytsia Luhanska, and the phenol sludge reservoir near Zalizne (formerly Artemove).

SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan called on the sides to prioritize the safety of the civilian population on 9 November. His full statement is available at: www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/355621
