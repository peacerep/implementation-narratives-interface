FACTS MATTER 

Between 17 and 30 May 2021, the SMM noted a decrease in the number of ceasefire vio- lations – recording 4,045 of them, compared with 4,848 in the previous two-week period.



The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, and observed people inside the latter two areas.

It also recorded 153 cease- fire violations and saw recent impact craters near former positions of the armed for- mations inside the disengagement area near Petrivske. 

Over the past two weeks, the SMM corroborated nine civilian casualties, including two fatalities.

This brings the total number of civilian casualties since the beginning of 2021 to 33 (11 fatalities and 22 injuries). 

During a five-day official visit to Ukraine, Secretary General Helga Maria Schmid stressed that “the SMM must be able to work safely, securely, and unhindered on both sides of the contact line.”

She also noted the continuing impact of landmines on the civilian popula- tion, as highlighted by the Mission’s latest thematic report.



The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 28 times, 27 of which in non- government-controlled areas, including once when members of the armed formations denied the SMM passage at a checkpoint near Olenivka, Donetsk region (see Spot Report 15/2021).



SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) were targeted by small-arms fire, five times in non -government-controlled areas and once in government-controlled areas.

The Mission’s UAVs experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming or jam- ming on 133 occasions on both sides of the contact line, including 30 instances when long- range UAVs were affected (forcing one emergency landing during which the aircraft sus- tained damage, see the Mission’s Spot Report 14/2021). 

The SMM continued to observe civilians crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line and the challenges they face.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska (open daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (open twice a week, as the armed formations’ corresponding checkpoint there is only open on Mondays and Fridays).



The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to ena- ble maintenance and operation of critical civilian infrastructure, including repairs to a phenol sludge reservoir near Zalizne (formerly Artemove) and to water infrastructure near Horlivka, benefitting over 250,000 civilians on both sides of the contact line.

On 20 May, despite security guarantees, an undetermined explosion occurred close to an SMM patrol near Avdiivka while it was facilitating the operation of the Donetsk Filtration Station.
