The SMM continued to monitor the situation at three crossing points at the administrative boundary line between the mainland and Crimea. The Mission monitors observed that the situation was calm and all three crossing points at Kalanchak, Chaplynka and Chonhar had re-opened. The SMM did not observe increased military movements at the three crossing points and in adjacent areas.

The SMM observed that the situation remained tense and volatile in Donetsk region with violence concentrated around the usual hotspots: Avdiivka-Yasynuvata -Donetsk airport triangle; western and northern outskirts of Horlivka; areas around Shyrokyne and Pikuzy (formerly Kominternove); and the Svitlodarsk-Debaltseve road.

In Luhansk region the SMM noted a relatively high level of violence, with areas around Stanytsia Luhanska and Pervomaisk-Popasna still the worst affected.

The SMM observed numerous weapons missing from storage sites and the presence of weapons in violation of withdrawal lines, quite often in and around residential areas. For instance, on 9 August it observed the presence of multiple- launch rocket systems in violation of the respective withdrawal line on both sides of the contact line.

Freedom of movement of SMM monitors continued to be severely restricted, particularly in areas not controlled by the Government.

Spot reports issued in this period noted SMM monitors being threatened at gunpoint near Lobacheve in Luhansk region (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/257546) and an explosion in Luhansk city allegedly targeting a senior “LPR” member (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/258051).
