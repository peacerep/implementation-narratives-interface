Facts Matter 

Between 20 August and 2 September, the SMM recorded fewer ceasefire violations (around 8,000), compared with the previous two weeks.

The SMM noted a decrease in the number of ceasefire violations following the recommitment to the ceasefire on the occasion of the beginning of the school year, which began at 00:01hrs on 29 August.

Until 2 September, however, it registered around 600 ceasefire violations.



The SMM continued to observe weapons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines (43 over the two-week period).



The SMM followed up on an explosion in the Donetsk city centre on the afternoon of 31 August.

Two people were reported dead, including a senior member of the armed formations, and at least nine other people were injured.



In addition to the ongoing threats posed by the presence of mines and unexploded ordnance – which the sides have undertaken to remove – the Mission continued to encounter restrictions of its freedom of movement.

This was particularly the case at checkpoints in non-government-controlled areas of the southern Donetsk region and in areas near the border with the Russian Federation outside government control.



The SMM followed up on temporary closures of entry-exit checkpoints, including near Maiorsk (due to armed violence in the area), Stanytsia Luhanska (due to construction work), as well as near Novotroitske and a checkpoint near Berezove (due to a wildfire in the area).



Civilians, particularly those living close to the contact line, continued to face hardship as a result of ongoing hostilities.

Between 1 January 2018 and 2 September 2018 the SMM corroborated 179 civilian casualties (34 killed and 145 injured).
