The OSCE SMM continued to monitor the security situation, registering a high number of ceasefire violations along the contact line in eastern Ukraine. Violations include the use of weapons proscribed by the Minsk agreements, namely mortars, artillery, tanks and multiple launch rocket systems.

The violence was concentrated around well-known hotspots, particularly in the Avdiivka-Yasynuvata-Donetsk airport area and in areas east and north-east of Mariupol. The situation in Luhansk region was relatively calm with limited violence in and around Stanytsia Luhanska, and in areas in the west of the region.

The humanitarian situation of the civilian population remains dire, with water and electricity shortages particularly evident in areas of Luhansk region not controlled by the Government. This is the result of the failure to resolve utility bill payment issues.

With the sides continuing to position and use hardware in and around residential areas, civilians invariably bear the brunt of continued shelling and other ceasefire violations. As well as causing civilian casualties and damaging homes and schools, critical civilian infrastructure has been badly affected, resulting in further disruptions to utility supplies.
