Latest News/Facts Matter 

The SMM noted a deterioration of the security situation in eastern Ukraine over the two week period, recording a marked increase in the number of ceasefire violations.



A spike in fighting around the Avdiivka-Yasynuvata-Donetsk airport area on 24 February was noted and the situation continued to be volatile and tense also in other hotspots.

In the week of 27 February to 5 March, the SMM monitored a significant increase in the number of ceasefire violations in the following areas:

east and north-east of Mariupol;

areas located between Popasna, Pervomaisk and Troitske;

the western and northern outskirts of Horlivka. 

From 20 to 26 February, the SMM observed 86 weapons in violation of withdrawal lines.

It has not observed any withdrawal of weapons from the security zone, despite repeated recommitments in this regard by the sides, including in February.

Unless the sides disengage and withdraw weapons, intense violence remains an ever-present danger.

In the week from 27 February to 5 March the use of Minsk-proscribed weapons, namely mortars, tanks, and artillery, increased by 230 per cent as compared with the previous week.

On 4 March the SMM facilitated local adherence to the ceasefire around the Donetsk Water Filtration Station, allowing for repair work and the resumption of water supply to residents in Avdiivka.

The following day, however, the station was shelled, resulting yet again in a halt to operations.



The SMM faced a series of security incidents in areas not controlled by the Government, some of which involved direct targeting of monitors and all of which impeded the Mission’s ability to monitor and report in accordance with its mandate.
