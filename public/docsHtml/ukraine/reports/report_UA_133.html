FACTS MATTER 

In the past two weeks, the SMM recorded an increase in the number of ceasefire viola- tions in eastern Ukraine as compared to the relatively low levels of violence observed over and immediately after Orthodox Easter.

Between 6 and 19 May, monitors recorded around 7,000 ceasefire violations per week (around 4,000 had been recorded the week before).



The second week of May saw a significant increase in the use of weapons that should have been withdrawn in accordance with the Minsk agreements (around 1,000 instanc- es).

In total, from 6 to 19 May monitors observed 277 weapons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines (216 in areas not controlled by the Government).

 Shelling and small-arms fire continued to impact civilian infrastructure.

In particular, the SMM observed fresh gunfire damage to two functioning schools in Troitske and in Zolote-5/Mykhailivka.

In Troitske, monitors were told that students had been at school when the damage occurred.

The school in Zolote has been hit nine times since Decem- ber 2018.

The area around the Donetsk Filtration Station, which supplies potable water to nearly 380,000 people on both sides of the contact line, continued to experience shelling.

In one instance on 15 May, the SMM, while facilitating and monitoring adher- ence to the ceasefire to enable the operation of the facility, heard three outgoing explo- sions less than 300 metres from their position.

 The Mission confirmed two civilian casualties, bringing the total number registered by the SMM in 2019 to 35 (six deaths and 29 injuries).

In one instance, a man died after a metal object he had found in the outskirts of Velyka Novosilka exploded.

 The SMM continued to observe people applying for passports of the Russian Federation in non-government-controlled areas of Donetsk region and started to observe the same in Luhansk region.

 The SMM continued to enable repairs to critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line.

In particular, repairs to power lines between Zolote-5/Mykhailivka and Yuzhna-Lomuvatka, and to almost 150 damaged houses in Marinka and Krasnohorivka.
