Latest News/Facts Matter 

The number of ceasefire violations registered by the Special Monitoring Mission (SMM) in Donetsk region remained high.

The use of mortar fire hit a new high with around 970 explosions recorded between 11-17 April.

Fighting concentrated around three hotspots:

the area between Avdiivka and Yasynuvata, and also Donetsk airport;

the northern and western outskirts of Horlivka;

and the area south and south-east of Svitlodarsk.



On 15 April in Luhansk region the SMM recorded an increase in ceasefire violations with more than 100 explosions around Stanytsia Luhanska. 

The SMM notes more and more weapons absent from the sites they were previously withdrawn to.

Monitors consistently see weapons in use and in violation of respective withdrawal lines - often positioned inside populated areas - on both sides of the contact line. 

The SMM’s freedom of movement is increasingly restricted.



Recently the SMM monitors faced serious security incidents:

an SMM vehicle was hit by a bullet near Snizhne on 7 April while hours later monitors were threatened at gunpoint near Smile (see:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/231901)

.

On 9 April an SMM patrol came under fire in Zhovanka (see:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/232326)

.



On 15 April the OSCE SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan expressed his dismay at the increase in fighting in the east of the country.

Read more at:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/233936
