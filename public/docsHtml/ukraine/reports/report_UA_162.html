Latest News/Facts Matter  The OSCE SMM welcomed the decision of the Trilateral Contact Group on disengagement of forces and hardware in three areas, namely Zolote, Stanytsia Luhanska and Petrivske.

In line with its mandate the SMM will monitor disengagement in these areas;

however, without access to the disengagement areas the SMM cannot verify adherence to the agreement.  Spot reports issued in this period noted an explosion 100m from the SMM near Stanytsia Luhanska bridge (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/264271) and small-arms fire near an SMM mini unmanned aerial vehicle near Bohorodychne (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/267526).



The OSCE SMM continued to monitor the ceasefire, noting the sides’ renewed commitment to adhere to the ceasefire on the occasion of the start of the new school year on 1 September.

Since then, the SMM noted a decrease in the level of violence, with occasional flare-ups, all of which result in a highly volatile situation.

The Donetsk airport-Yasynuvata-Avdiivka triangle remained the epicentre of violence.

The situation was generally calm in the Luhansk region.

 Risk factors remained:

weapons in violation of withdrawal lines;

mines, unexploded ordnance and explosive remnants of war present;

armed forces and formations positioned dangerously close to one another.

 Freedom of movement of SMM monitors continued to be restricted, particularly in areas not controlled by the Government.
