Latest News/Facts Matter 

Between 25 June and 8 July, the OSCE SMM recorded fewer ceasefire violations along the contact line – around 9,700.

This represents a 27 per cent decrease compared with the previous two weeks.

The decrease in ceasefire violations was accompanied by a reduction in the number of civilian casualties;

the SMM confirmed that three civilians had been injured compared with 15 injured in the previous reporting period. 

The reduction in the level of violence followed the renewed commitment to the ceasefire, on the occasion of the summer harvest.

The recommitment was agreed in Minsk on 27 June and came into effect at 00:01 on 1 July. 

The Special Representative of the OSCE Chairperson-in-Office in Ukraine and in the Trilateral Contact Group Martin Sajdik and SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan welcomed the recommitment to cease fire on 6 July.

But they noted that it needed to be matched by a concrete set of actions to be sustainable including the withdrawal of heavy weapons (www.osce.org/chairmanship/386996). 

The Mission continued to observe violations of key provisions of the Minsk agreements and related commitments.

In particular, it observed weapons in violation of withdrawal lines on both sides of the contact line – 107 in government- and 157 in non-government-controlled areas.

In addition, it observed the presence of newly laid mines on both sides of the contact line and the presence of armed forces and formations in close proximity to each other as well as in residential areas.
