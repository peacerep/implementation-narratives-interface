FACTS MATTER 

Between 22 January and 4 February, the security situation in eastern Ukraine remained tense and volatile.

During the two-week reporting period, the SMM recorded around 11,000 ceasefire violations in total – a 15 per cent decrease compared to the previous two weeks.

There was, however, an uptick in armed violence in the latter half of this period, with 25 per cent increase in ceasefire violations compared to the previous week.



The vast majority of violations were concentrated in five hotspots:

the Avdiivka-Yasynuvata- Donetsk airport area, the areas located south-west, south and south-east of Svitlodarsk, the Popasna-Pervomaisk area, the eastern and north-eastern sector of Mariupol and the western and south-western outskirts of Horlivka.



Between 22 January and 4 February, the SMM confirmed eight civilian casualties, which brings the total number since the beginning of 2018 to 14 persons:

four killed and ten injured.



The SMM faced 24 restrictions to its freedom of movement:

3 times in government-controlled areas and 21 times in areas outside government control.

On 2 and 3 February, while conducting mini-UAV flights in government-controlled areas the SMM heard small-arms fire assessed as directed towards its unmanned aerial vehicles 

On 2 February, an SMM patrol in a non-government-controlled area near Yasynuvata heard the hissing sound of three bullets flying west to east over their heads and saw one of the bullets land 10-15m east of location.

Between 22 January and 4 February, the Mission facilitated and monitored 11 “windows of silence” – localized adherence to the ceasefire – which allowed for repairs and maintenance work on two critical infrastructure facilities on both sides of the contact line.

They include water wells near non-government-controlled Krasnyi Lyman and at the Petrivske pumping station near government-controlled Artema.



On 31 January, Angelino Alfano, the OSCE Chairperson-in-Office and Italian Minister of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation and Ertugrul Apakan, Ambassador, Chief Monitor of the OSCE SMM visited Mariupol area to learn more about security situation and the Mission's activities in eastern Ukraine.

They also met with the Ukrainian Minister of Foreign Affairs Pavlo Klimkin and other officials.
