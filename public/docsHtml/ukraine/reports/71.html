Despite claims by all sides of heavy weapons withdrawal, the SMM observed on a daily basis the presence and/or use of such weapons (e.g. main battle tanks (T-72, T-64), howitzers, anti-aircraft systems (Strela-10).

The SMM observed and heard continued fighting in the areas in and around “Donetsk People’s Republic” (“DPR”)-controlled Donetsk airport; the area of “DPR”-controlled Horlivka and Debaltseve and in Shyrokyne.

In the morning of 3 June fighting erupted around the government-controlled town of Marinka (23km west-south-west of Donetsk city centre). See the Spot report here: www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/162116

Meanwhile in the Luhansk region the ceasefire violations concentrated in the government-controlled towns of Stanytsia Luhanska, Nyzhnie, Shchastia.

On 18-22 May the OSCE Special Representative and Co-ordinator for Combating Trafficking in Human Beings, Ambassador Madina Jarbussynova, visited Kharkiv and Dnipropetrovsk.

On 26 May the SMM confirmed the deaths of three civilians by shelling in Horlivka and saw damage consistent with an artillery strike.

On 2 June in Minsk the SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug attended another round of meetings coordinating the work of the working group on security issues under the auspices of the Trilateral Contact Group .

The SMM is restrained in fulfilling its monitoring functions by restrictions imposed by third parties and security considerations.
