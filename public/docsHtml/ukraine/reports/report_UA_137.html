Latest News/Facts Matter  Between 8 and 21 March 2021, the SMM recorded 1,260 ceasefire viola-tions, compared with 944 in the previous two-week period.  The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, observing people in the latter two areas and recording one ceasefire violation inside the one near Petrivske. 

During the two-week reporting period, the SMM corroborated three civilian casualties, including two fatalities.

This brings the total number of civilian casualties that occurred since the beginning of 2021 to four (two fatalities and two injuries).

 The Mission observed trench extensions on both sides of the contact line.

 The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to enable repairs to and maintenance and operation of critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line, including to enable emergency repairs to a power substation in Oleksandrivka (benefiting around 4,500 civilians) and to complete repairs to power lines near Novo-luhanske and Dolomitne (benefitting about 3,700 civilians).

 The SMM continued to observe civilians facing challenges when crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska (operational daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (twice a week as the armed formations’ checkpoint there is only operational on Mondays and Fridays).  The Mission’s freedom of movement on the ground was restricted six times, all in non-government-controlled areas (see Spot Report 3/2021).

SMM un-manned aerial vehicles were targeted four times by small-arms fire, twice in government-controlled and twice in non-government-controlled areas;

and experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming on 30 occasions while flying on both sides of the contact line.
