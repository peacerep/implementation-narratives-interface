Latest News/Facts Matter  Following a 40% reduction in the level of violence from 15 to 21 May, the SMM observed a worsening of the security situation with a 45% increase in ceasefire violations recorded over the period from 22 to 28 May.  The SMM monitors continue to face threats while implementing their duties.

For example, on 17 May, a smoke-generating device was thrown from a military-type truck close to an SMM vehicle near so-called “DPR”-controlled Dokuchaievsk.

No SMM personnel were injured or assets damaged.

See:

www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/317891  Civilians continue to suffer as armed men, soldiers and military hardware are regularly observed in residential areas and in proximity to civilian infrastructure.

Even with decreased violence levels from 15 to 21 May, the SMM confirmed six civilian casualties, one of whom was killed.

On 22-28 May, the SMM confirmed nine civilian casualties, all injured by shelling.

This raises the civilian casualty figures confirmed by the SMM to 233, including 44 fatalities since the beginning of 2017.  On 28-31 May, the SMM hosted 27 Ambassadors to the OSCE.

They visited Ukraine to reaffirm their support to resolving the crisis in and around the country, and travelled to Kyiv, Kramatorsk and Kharkiv where they voiced support for the SMM and assessed the effects of the conflict on the ground and its impact on the humanitarian situation.
