FACTS MATTER 

Between 10 and 23 December 2018, the SMM confirmed that three civilians were injured.

This brings the total number of civilian casualties since 1 January 2018 to 225 (43 deaths and 182 injuries).



The SMM recorded about 13,600 ceasefire violations (as compared with over 20,000 in the previous two-week period) and 221 weapons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines (36 in areas not controlled by the Government).



The Mission continued to observe positions, hardware, personnel and other indications of military presence, and recorded around 200 ceasefire violations inside the Zolote disengagement area.

Residents of the Vilnyi area of Zolote-4/Rodina, immediately east- north-east of the disengagement area, told the SMM that, due to the worsening security situation, many residents had relocated to government-controlled Zolote-1/Soniachnyi. 

The SMM continued to observe long queues of people waiting to cross the contact line near Stanytsia Luhanska.

In addition to often lengthy security checks, civilians passing through what is the only crossing point in Luhansk region must walk about 2km between forward positions and at one point, where the now destroyed Stanytsia Luhanska bridge stands, along often snow-covered, slippery wooden ramps.



The Mission continued to face restrictions to its freedom of movement and other impediments to the implementation of its mandate.

Mines and unexploded ordnance continued to restrict the Mission’s access to all three disengagement areas and elsewhere.

In addition, the SMM’s freedom of movement was restricted on 28 occasions;

24 times in areas not controlled by the Government, mostly in areas close to the border with the Russian Federation and along the coast of the Azov Sea.

Small-arms fire was directed at SMM unmanned aerial vehicles on five occasions.
