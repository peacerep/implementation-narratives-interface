For the fourteenth week, the security situation remains relatively calm following addi-tional measures to strengthen the ceasefire, which took effect on 27 July. Between 19 October and 1 November, the SMM recorded 253 ceasefire violations, compared with 215 in the previous two-week period.

The Mission continued monitoring the security situation in and around the three pilot disengagement areas, recording no ceasefire violations inside any of them, but noting the regular presence of people within, or close to, former military and military-type positions inside the areas near Petrivske and Zolote.

During the past weeks, SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) spotted new trenches and extensions to existing trenches in areas between government-controlled Vodiane and non-government-controlled Uzhivka.

The SMM corroborated reports of five civilian casualties, including two fatalities. Four of the casualties were due to the detonation of explosive devices and one, occurring on 20 March in Donetsk city, due to shelling. This brings the total number of civilian casualties since the beginning of 2020 to 92: 16 fatalities and 76 injuries.

The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to ena-ble repairs to and maintenance of critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the con-tact line. This included repairs to water infrastructure near Pankivka and Krasnyi Lyman providing water to around 250,000 civilians, and inspection of power lines near Zolote-4/Rodina, providing electricity to several thousand civilians. In Luhansk region, the Mission noted that the entry-exit checkpoint (EECP) in Stanytsia Luhanska has remained closed since 15 October due to COVID-19 mitigation measures while the corresponding checkpoint of the armed formations remained partially opera-tional. In Donetsk region, where EECPs remain operational, the only corresponding oper-ational checkpoint of the armed formations is the one near Olenivka. It is open only two days a week, resulting in severely restricted movement and hardship for the civilian pop-ulation.

The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted seven times on the ground, all in non-government-controlled areas. SMM UAVs experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming on 22 occasions while flying on both sides of the contact line, including twice over the disengagement area near Petrivske and once close to the disengagement area near Stanytsia Luhanska.

Crossing difficulties at the contact line continue to hinder the SMM’s monitoring of non-government-controlled areas.
