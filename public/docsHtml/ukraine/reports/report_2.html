LATEST NEWS 

The SMM observed a marked decrease in violence after the sides renewed their commitment to the ceasefire for the new school year starting from 1 September and generally recorded double-digit numbers of ceasefire viola- tions.



However, there was a spike in violence on 10 September, with 515 explosions recorded, mostly in the Donetsk airport-Yasynuvata-Avdiivka triangle.



The situation has remained generally calm in Luhansk region.



On 7 September an OSCE SMM vehicle was set on fire in Ivano-Frankivsk.

Risk factors remained, with weapons in violation of withdrawal lines;

mines, unexploded ordnance and remnants of war present;

armed forces and formations precariously positioned close to one another.



The sides continued to restrict SMM freedom of movement, mostly in areas not controlled by the Government, and placed other impediments in its way.

SMM auxiliary camera equipment, for example, was stolen and destroyed at the “DPR”-controlled Oktiabr mine on 3 September.
