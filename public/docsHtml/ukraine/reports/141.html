The security situation in eastern Ukraine has slowly but steadily deteriorated despite the sides’ recommitment to the ceasefire in late August. Notably, during the week of 23 to 29 October the SMM recorded more than 5,000 ceasefire violations, an increase of 80 per cent over the previous week. Most violations were recorded in the areas of Avdiivka-Yasynuvata-Donetsk airport and Svitlodarsk-Debaltseve.

At the same time, the Mission recorded a decrease of more than 50 per cent in the use of Minsk-proscribed weapons in the week of 23 to 29 October, compared to the week before.

From 16 to 29 October, the Mission confirmed nine civilian casualties, including one fatality. A 25-year-old man died from a bullet wound, which he had sustained in the Trudivski area of Donetsk city. This brings the total number of civilian casualties recorded this year to 411.

The SMM, in co-ordination with the Joint Centre for Control and Co-ordination, also contributed to the improvement of the humanitarian situation by facilitating and monitoring localized adherence to the ceasefire. From 16 to 29 October, these “windows of silence” allowed for inspection, repair and maintenance work to 15 infrastructure facilities.

In the past two weeks the SMM facilitated and monitored repairs and maintenance work to the water wells near Krasnyi Lyman, the power lines in Druzhba, the Donetsk Filtration Station, the thermal power plant in Shchastia, the Petrivske water pumping station, and other essential civilian infrastructure. In total, the Mission conducted at least 32 patrols in support of repairs and maintenance of infrastructure facilities.

The SMM’s access remained restricted in multiple locations on both sides of the contact line, in particular in areas outside of government control close to the border with the Russian Federation. At a “DPR”-controlled checkpoint near Verkhnoshyrokivske armed men in military-style clothing pointed a heavy machine-gun at an SMM patrol: www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/352556.

The SMM has also monitored public gatherings across the country, including one in front of the national parliament building in Kyiv.
