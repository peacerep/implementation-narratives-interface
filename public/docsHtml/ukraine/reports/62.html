The SMM recorded an approximate 20 per cent decrease in violence between 27 March and 2 April compared with the previous week, and a calmer situation since the renewed commitment to adhere to the ceasefire beginning on 1 April. At the same time, the security situation remained volatile and unpredictable.

From 27 March to 2 April, the Mission observed an almost 90 per cent decrease in the use of heavy weapons. But in the preceding week, from 20 to 26 March, the SMM recorded the second highest use of proscribed weapons since the Addendum to Package of Measures came into force, including at least 3,930 explosions.

The SMM followed up on the electricity outage at the Donetsk Water Filtration Station (DFS) and in Avdiivka on 30 March (see: www.osce.org/special-monitoringmission-to-ukraine/309051). It facilitated and monitored de-mining activities and repairs at the damaged power lines in the area of the DFS, which were completed on 4 April.

From 23 March to 5 April, the Mission faced three serious incidents involving smallarms fire. Two cases occurred in the “DPR”-controlled areas: on 28 March and 2 April in Kozatske and Sakhanka, respectively, where armed men opened fire close to the SMM patrols (see: www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/308426; www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/309116).

On 26 March, in government-controlled Orikhove-Donetske small-arms fire was directed at the SMM’s UAV (see: www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/307681). No SMM personnel were injured and no assets damaged.
