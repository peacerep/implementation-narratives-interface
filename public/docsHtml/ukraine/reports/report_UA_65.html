Latest News/Facts Matter 

Between 10 and 23 February, the SMM recorded about 13,100 ceasefire violations, compared with about 8,500 in the previous two-week period.

Most of the violence was recorded in the latter part of the reporting period, with 3,000 ceasefire violations, in-cluding 2,500 explosions, recorded on 18 February in areas around non-government-controlled Berezivske, Holubivka (formerly Kirovsk), Holubivske and Donetskyi, and gov-ernment-controlled Orikhove and Novotoshkivske, all in Luhansk region.



The Mission continued monitoring the security situation in and around the three disen-gagement areas.

It observed a calm situation inside the disengagement areas near Stan-ytsia Luhanska and Zolote;

it recorded ceasefire violations inside the one near Petrivske. 

The SMM recorded 67 weapons in violation of their respective withdrawal lines, com-pared to 27 in the previous two-week period:

17 in government-controlled areas and 50 in areas outside government control.



The Mission confirmed reports of four civilian casualties, bringing the total number rec-orded since the beginning of the year to 14:

one killed and 13 injured. 

The SMM facilitated and monitored adherence to localized ceasefires to enable repairs to and maintenance of critical civilian infrastructure, which provides utilities to more than 1.5 million civilians on both sides of the contact line.

These included repairs to the South Donbas water pipeline between government-controlled Pervomaiske and Pisky, and repairs to damaged power lines in non-government-controlled Donetskyi, Holu-bivske and Zolote-5/Mykhailivka. 

The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 49 times (all in non-government-controlled areas, predominantly in southern Donetsk region).

SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) were subjected to signal interference on both sides of the contact line on 45 occasions and were targeted by small-arms fire once in a non-government-controlled area.
