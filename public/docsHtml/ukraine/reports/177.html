FACTS MATTER 

Between 9 and 22 March, the SMM recorded about 11,300 ceasefire violations, including about 5,350 explosions.

This compares with about 9,100 and 3,150, re- spectively, in the previous two-week period.



The Mission continued monitoring the security situation in and around the three disengagement areas.

The SMM observed a calm situation inside the disengage- ment area near Stanytsia Luhanska.

It recorded ceasefire violations inside the dis- engagement area near Petrivske, and it observed mines and unexploded ordnance inside the disengagement area near Zolote.



Over the two-week period, the SMM recorded 85 weapons in violation of their respective withdrawal lines, compared with 92 in the previous two-week period:

34 in government-controlled areas and 51 in non-government-controlled areas.



SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) spotted anti-tank mines on both sides of the contact line, including about 640 near Sanzharivka and Polove, and 400 near Pyshchevyk. 

The Mission confirmed four civilian casualties:

one fatality and three injuries, bringing the total since the beginning of the year to 25 (three killed and 22 in- jured). 

The Mission continued to monitor preventive measures related to the COVID-19 outbreak at entry-exit checkpoints (EECP), observing staff wearing protective masks and gloves, and measuring the temperature of civilians passing through.

It also noted members of the armed formations wearing protective glasses and masks, and screening pedestrians transiting through a number of corresponding checkpoints.

Initially the SMM observed long queues at EECPs and corresponding checkpoints.

Towards the end of the reporting period, the SMM noted that pas- sage for civilians across the contact line in both directions was largely denied.



During the reporting period, the Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 68 times.

Sixty-five instances occurred in areas not controlled by the Government, mostly in southern Donetsk region and at border crossing points in Luhansk region.

A number of restrictions occurred at the contact line, along official crossing routes (see Spot Report 2/2020).

SMM UAVs were subjected to signal interference on both sides of the contact line on 30 occasions, resulting in the loss of a mid-range UAV inside the Petrivske disengagement area.

SMM UAVs were targeted by small- arms fire six times (twice in government-controlled areas and four times in non- government-controlled areas).
