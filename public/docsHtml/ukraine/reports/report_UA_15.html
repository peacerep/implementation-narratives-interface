Latest News/Facts Matter 

Over the last two weeks, the SMM registered a decrease in the number of ceasefire viola-tions and considerably fewer cases of the use of weapons that should have been with-drawn in accordance with the Minsk agreements.

Between 7 and 13 August, the SMM recorded about 15 % fewer ceasefire violations compared with the previous week.

From 14 to 20 August, the number of ceasefire violations decreased by some 45 % compared with the week before.



Violence resulted in a number of civilian casualties with two people killed and seven in-jured from 7 to 20 August.

This brought the total number of civilian casualties confirmed by the SMM in 2017 to 357, including 66 fatalities. 

The SMM’s freedom of movement continued to be restricted due to the presence of mines and unexploded ordnance, in particular at Shchastia Bridge and on the road be-tween Pikuzy and Mariupol.

The Joint Centre for Control and Co-ordination has, however, on some occasions successfully co-ordinated between the sides to ensure de-mining, notably along the Svitlodarsk-Debaltseve road.



Continued attacks on technical monitoring equipment, namely unmanned aerial vehicles and cameras, also impacted the Mission’s ability to monitor.

On 10 August, the Mission’s newly installed thermal camera at the Donetsk Filtration Station near Yasynuvata was destroyed by small-arms fire.

The SMM Chief Monitor condemned the incident (see:

www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/335046

).



Threats and intimidation – used also to undermine the Mission’s work – continued:

on 13 August an armed man at Stanytsia Luhanska bridge threatened to shoot SMM patrol members (see www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/335566).
