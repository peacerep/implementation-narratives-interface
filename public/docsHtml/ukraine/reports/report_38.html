Latest News 

One SMM patrol member died and two members were taken to hospital on 23 April after their vehicle was severely damaged by an explosion close to “LPR”-controlled Pryshyb, possibly after coming into contact with a mine (see:

www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-toukraine/312971).



Following the incident, SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan issued a statement (see:

www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-toukraine/312986

), while on 28 April, the OSCE Permanent Council expressed their support for the SMM and called for a swift investigation (see:

www.osce.org/chairmanship/314331).



The three SMM casualties are among 154 civilians killed or injured by mines or unexploded ordnance in incidents recorded by the SMM since the beginning of 2016.

No one has been held accountable. 

The SMM’s operations in Donetsk and Luhansk regions remained restricted following the fatal incident of 23 April near Pryshyb;

these restrictions continued to limit the Mission’s observations.



Two additional security incidents occurred on 28 April, both in areas controlled by the so-called “LPR”, in which an explosion and small-arms fire occurred close to SMM monitors (see:

www.osce.org/specialmonitoring-mission-to-ukraine/314456)

.

No SMM personnel were injured or assets were damaged.



The Mission has continued to observe violence along the contact line, with hundreds of explosions recorded daily.

Weapons proscribed by the Minsk agreements are observed by the SMM on a regular basis in violation of withdrawal lines.
