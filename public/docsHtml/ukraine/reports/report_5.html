Latest News/Facts Matter 

Between 9 and 22 July, the SMM noted a deterioration of the security situation in eastern Ukraine.

In the second week of the re-commitment to the ceasefire, the Mission recorded an increase in the number of ceasefire violations.



From 9 to 22 July, the SMM also confirmed that at least eight people were injured.

One of them – a 60-year-old man – was hit by a bullet in front of his house in Krasnohorivka. 

The Mission continued to observe weaponry and other indications of military presence in residential areas on both sides of the contact line, for example in Novozvanivka and Korsun.



The SMM facilitated the operation of the Donetsk Filtration Station by monitoring the security situation around the station.

The SMM also facilitated and monitored repairs to critical infrastructure on both sides of the contact line, including to the phenol sludge reservoir near Zalizne. 

In other regions, the SMM continued to follow up on incidents targeting the Roma community.

The Mission monitored a parade aimed at promoting LGBT rights in Kryvyi Rih and other public gatherings and selected court hearings.



The Chief Monitor addressed the OSCE Permanent Council in Vienna on 19 July and called upon the sides to take measures to alleviate human suffering:

www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/388682.



The Mission became aware of media reports about an alleged breach of its security and published a statement in relation to these allegations:

www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/388286.
