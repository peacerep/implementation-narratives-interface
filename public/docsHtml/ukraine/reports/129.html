The OSCE Special Monitoring Mission to Ukraine (SMM) continued to observe violence in many parts of Donbas. Reflecting a high degree of volatility and unpredictability, the SMM on some days recorded fewer than ten explosions whilst on others, over 100.

The Yasynuvata-Avdiivka area north of Donetsk city and areas south-east of Svitlodarsk remained hotspots, with areas around Donetsk airport again becoming more tense.

Luhansk region remained relatively calm, with sporadic violence in the region’s west and occasional intense fighting immediately south of Stanytsia Luhanska.

Weapons continued to be observed in the security zone. Significant numbers of weapons were still missing from holding areas and permanent storage sites.

On 20 May in violation of the Package of Measures for the Implementation of the Minsk Agreements, armed “DPR” members cut the power supply to an SMM repeater device in Donetsk city, thereby incapacitating SMM remote monitoring equipment at “DPR”-controlled Oktiabr mine and in government- controlled Avdiivka.

On 24-30 May the OSCE SMM Principal Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug is visiting eastern Ukraine to assess the security situation and encourage all concerned to work towards peace and normalization of the situation in Ukraine.

On 25 May the SMM opened a new Forward Patrol Base in Shchastia, Luhansk region, which is the Mission’s ninth such base in Donbas. Read more at: www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/242621
