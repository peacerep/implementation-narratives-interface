The Deputy Chief Monitor of the SMM, Alexander Hug, visited southeast Ukraine on 9-10 April to encourage parties to further disengage in Shyrokyne and to work towards normalization and demilitarization in the town and surrounding areas.



On 12 April Chief Monitor of the SMM, Ambassador Ertugrul Apakan, expressed his dismay at the resumed violence in Shyrokyne following three days of calm in Shyrokyne facilitated by the SMM.

He called upon all sides to exercise maximum restraint and to honour the commitments enshrined in the Minsk agreements. 

Following the escalation of violence in Shyrokyne over the Orthodox Easter weekend, SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug and his team travelled back to Mariupol on 13 April to spearhead efforts for a localised ceasefire.

Since 13 April the SMM has visited Shyrokyne on a daily basis, engaging the two sides in a dialogue aimed at the cessation of hostilities.



On 16 April the SMM resumed facilitating access for MH17 investigators to the crash site. 
