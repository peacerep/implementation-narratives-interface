FACTS MATTER  Between 23 March and 5 April, the SMM recorded about 8,700 ceasefire viola- tions, including about 2,700 explosions.

This constitutes a 23% and 50% reduction, respectively, on the previous two-week period.

 The Mission continued monitoring the security situation in and around the three disengagement areas.

The SMM observed a calm situation inside the disengage- ment areas near Stanytsia Luhanska and Zolote.

It again recorded ceasefire viola- tions inside the disengagement area near Petrivske.

 The SMM recorded 25 weapons in violation of their respective withdrawal lines, compared with 89 in the previous two-week period:

10 in government-controlled areas and 15 in non-government-controlled areas.

 The Mission confirmed three civilian casualties (one fatality and two injuries), bringing the total since the beginning of the year to 28 (four killed and 24 injured).

 The SMM continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to enable repairs to and maintenance of critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line.

The Mission also facilitated activities related to demining near Krasnyi Lyman and Vyskryva, as well as at two cemeteries in Katerynivka and Zolote-4/Rodina ahead of Easter. 

In light of the COVID-19 outbreak, the SMM noted that entry-exit checkpoints (EECP) and corresponding checkpoints of the armed formations remained closed to civilian traffic.

The Mission observed civilians attempting to travel through the contact line in both directions.

 Members of the armed formations continued to deny the SMM passage at check- points along official crossing routes in Donetsk and Luhansk regions.

Overall, the Mission’s freedom of movement on the ground was restricted 39 times (all but one in areas not controlled by the Government).

SMM UAVs were subjected to signal interference on both sides of the contact line on 15 occasions, and were targeted by small-arms fire seven times (four in government-controlled areas, three in non-government-controlled areas).
