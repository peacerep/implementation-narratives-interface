There were continued violations of the ceasefire by both sides. The OSCE Special Monitoring Mission to Ukraine (SMM) recorded the most violent breaches west of Donetsk city and around Donetsk airport, in and around Horlivka – in particular, in the Svitlodarsk/Debaltseve area – and in areas north- east of Mariupol, around Kominternove. In Luhansk region, the overall security situation remained relatively calm, with daily ceasefire violations numbering in the low double-digits – most of these were attributable to live-fire exercises.

The SMM noted a slight decrease in the use of artillery and mortars. Worryingly, however, monitors observed a greater use of multiple launch rocket systems and noted their presence within the respective withdrawal lines as defined by the Minsk Package of Measures.

The Mission noted an increased presence of other proscribed weapons inside the security zone. The SMM also continued to record weapons as missing from permanent storage sites and heavy weapons holding areas.

The SMM Forward Patrol Base in Stanytsia Luhanska is temporarily not operational due to security concerns.

On 3 March the Chief Monitor of the OSCE SMM Ertugrul Apakan welcomed the decisions on mine action and the prohibition of live-fire exercises by the Trilateral Contact Group. See: www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/225566. The SMM will impartially and objectively monitor and report on the progress made in implementing these decisions.
