Between 19 March and 1 April 2018, the security situation in the east of Ukraine remained tense and continued to gradually deteriorate. Overall, the number of ceasefire violations in the two-week reporting period reached almost 10,000 (compared to 4,300 in the previous two weeks). This number includes more than 1,000 ceasefire violations recorded in the three days following a renewed commitment to the ceasefire that took effect at 00:01 (Kyiv time) on 30 March.

The OSCE SMM’s freedom of movement remained restricted due to the presence of mines and unexploded ordnance on both sides of the contact line, as well as due to the denial of access by the sides (mainly in areas not controlled by the Government). For instance, on 23 March, small arms were fired near an SMM patrol in Pikuzy and the SMM heard impacts assessed as ricochets of two bullets 5-10m north of its position. On 30 March, three SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) were fired at over non-government-controlled areas.

On 28 March, the Mission re-introduced the use of long-range UAVs. With a range of over 160km, the SMM’s long-range UAVs will operate over Donetsk and Luhansk regions equally on both sides of the contact line, including at night when ground patrolling is restricted due to security considerations.

Violence in the east of Ukraine resulted in a number of civilian casualties. From the beginning of 2018 to 1 April the Mission corroborated 37 civilian casualties, including 8 killed.

The Mission continued to facilitate access for workers to and from the Donetsk Filtration Station. The SMM also facilitated repairs to the Petrivske pumping station near Artema.
