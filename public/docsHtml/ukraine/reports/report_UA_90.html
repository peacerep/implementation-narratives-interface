Matter 

Donetsk region was dangerously volatile during the reporting period with the first week (20-26 June) seeing a 35 per cent decrease in violence on the previous week.

The second week (27 June – 3 July) saw a 150 per cent increase in violence on the preceding week.

Luhansk region remained relatively calm.



Violence was concentrated around four main hotspots:

the AvdiivkaYasynuvata-Donetsk airport triangle;

the area around Shyrokyne, where the use of multiple launch rocket systems was recorded;

the northern and western outskirts of Horlivka;

and along the Svitlodarsk-Debaltseve road, where a sharp spike in daytime fighting was noted. 

On 22 June monitors from the SMM were caught in mortar shelling in Vodiane (see Spot Report at:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/248141)

.



The SMM observed long queues of civilians at checkpoints near the contact line.

Shelling in residential areas resulted in injuries and fatalities among civilians, with two young boys found dead in Kominternove on 26 June (see Daily Report at www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/248801).



The Mission continued to be subjected to freedom-of-movement restrictions, the majority of which were imposed in areas not controlled by the Government, including in areas along the Ukrainian border with the Russian Federation.
