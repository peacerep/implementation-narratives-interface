In the Donetsk and Luhansk regions the overall situation along the contact line remained tense.

The on-going fighting continues to take a substantial toll on the civilian population. Certain residential areas are regularly shelled creating daily fear for people’s lives.

On 23 June OSCE SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug took part in Deutsche Welle Global Media Forum in Bonn where he participated in panel “24/7 news cycle. How can international organisations adapt?”

On 27 June the SMM verified press reports that there are no civilians remaining in Shyrokyne.

OSCE SMM issued two thematic reports: “Freedom of movement across the administrative boundary line with Crimea” (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/165691) and “Gender Dimensions of SMM’s Monitoring: One Year of Progress” (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/165806)

On 13 July in Vienna for the seventh time OSCE SMM Chief Monitor Ambassador Apakan will address the OSCE Permanent Council on the situation in Ukraine and activities carried out by the SMM in responding to the crisis.
