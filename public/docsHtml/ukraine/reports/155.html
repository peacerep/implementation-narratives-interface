On 29 September participants of the Trilateral Contact Group’s working group on security issues finalized their work on a long-awaited Addendum to the February Package of Measures. The Addendum is a plan for withdrawal to a distance of 15km for tanks, artillery pieces up to 100mm in calibre, and mortars up to and including 120mm. SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan welcomed the agreement in a statement the following day.

On 1-3 October OSCE SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug visited Luhansk to assess the general security situation in the region and meet with the SMM team. The SMM patrol with Hug attempted to reach the international border but was prevented from proceeding by “LPR” “border guards”. Hug also met with interlocutors to solicit support for opening the SMM Forward Patrol Bases in the Luhansk region.

On 4 October the SMM started to monitor the withdrawal of weapons as required under the Addendum to the Package of Measures.

The ceasefire largely continued to hold, with few incidents recorded, most of which were assessed to have involved the use of small-arms or to have likely been part of training or demining activities. Heavy weapons, however, remained ever-present, constituting a risk of escalation.

The SMM held a workshop for its newly-established Kyiv Youth Contact Group on 26-28 September. As a result of the workshop the participants presented two projects to foster dialogue in Kyiv and in eastern Ukraine.
