For the 24th consecutive week, the security situation remained relatively calm following the additional measures to strengthen the ceasefire that took effect on 27 July 2020. Between 14 December 2020 and 10 January 2021, the Special Monitoring Mission (SMM) recorded 2,123 ceasefire violations. For comparison, during a similar four-week period between 14 December 2019 and 10 January 2020, the Mission recorded about 15,500 ceasefire violations.

The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske. It observed people inside the latter two areas during daytime hours and recorded ceasefire violations inside the area near Petrivske for the first time since August 2020.

During the four-week reporting period, the SMM corroborated reports of 12 civilian casualties. Of these, nine were caused by explosive objects. The other three casualties resulted from shelling or small-arms fire, and they occurred be-fore additional measures took effect on 27 July 2020. The total number of civilian casualties in 2020 stands at 128: 23 fatalities and 105 injuries.

The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized cease-fires to enable repairs to and maintenance and operation of critical civilian infra-structure, infrastructure near Novotoshkivske, Berezove, Pivdenne, Zolote-5/Mykhailivka and Krasnyi Lyman, which altogether provide water to more than 165,000 people. including water

The SMM continued to observe civilians facing challenges when crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line in both directions. It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use continued to be Stanytsia Luhanska and Novotroitske/Olenivka. In each location, only a limited number of civilians travelling in both directions were observed.
