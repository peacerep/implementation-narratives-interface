FACTS MATTER  Between 11 and 24 January 2021, the Special Monitoring Mission (SMM) record- ed 966 ceasefire violations, compared with 986 in the previous two-week period.

On 23 January, the Mission noted a peak of ceasefire violations – 358 – mostly near the disengagement area near Petrivske – the highest number recorded on a single day since the additional measures took effect on 27 July 2020.

 The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, and observed people in the latter two areas dur- ing the daytime.

While outside the area near Zolote, the Mission heard small- arms fire, assessed as targeting an SMM unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV).

 During the two-week reporting period, the SMM corroborated two civilian casu- alties – the case of a woman killed by the detonation of an explosive device in September 2020 in Makiivka, Donetsk region and a man injured due to an explo- sion of an unknown object near Brianka, Luhansk region in May 2020.

 The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized cease- fires to enable repairs to and maintenance and operation of critical civilian infra- structure, including to water infrastructure near Pivdenne and Slovianoserbsk, providing water to around 150,000 civilians on both sides of the contact line.

 The Mission continued seeing anti-tank mines on both sides of the contact line, some for the first time, including 15 mines, part of a larger minefield, in areas near Krasnohorivka, and eight mines laid across a road on the outskirts of Do- netsk city’s Petrovskyi district.

 The SMM continued to observe civilians facing challenges when crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line in both directions.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use continued to be Stanytsia Luhanska (operational daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (checkpoint of the armed formations near Olenivka, operational only on Monday and Friday).

 The Mission’s freedom of movement on the ground was restricted 14 times, all in non-government-controlled areas.

SMM UAVs were targeted five times by small-arms fire in non-government-controlled areas and twice in government- controlled areas and experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming on 23 occasions while flying on both sides of the contact line.
