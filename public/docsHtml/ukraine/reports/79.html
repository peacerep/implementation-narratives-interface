Despite a decrease in ceasefire violations during the period from 13 to 26 June, the security situation in eastern Ukraine remained volatile. The SMM recorded about 1,000 ceasefire violations over the weekend of 24/25 June. The violence resulted in a high number of civilian casualties, with five people killed and six injured during the reporting period. This brings the total number of civilian casualties confirmed by the SMM in 2017 to 285, including 52 fatalities.

The safety and security of SMM monitors was interfered with on two occasions. On 20 June, as monitors were patrolling close to the so-called “DPR”-controlled Yasynuvata railway station, an armed man pointed an assault rifle at them, then tried to smash the windows of their vehicle and subsequently fired shots at the vehicle as it departed from the scene (see: www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/324391). On 23 June, in government-controlled Zolote, bullets, mortars and grenades were fired approximately 100m from SMM patrol members, with some bullets flying close overhead (see: www.osce.org/special-monitoring-mission-toukraine/325376).

The SMM continued to note long queues of civilians waiting long hours to travel in both directions on the Stanytsia Luhanska bridge. On 21 June, SMM monitors observed additional damage to the bridge, with people falling as a result.

The SMM observed water shortages in several settlements affected by damage to a water pumping station near Vasylivka, as well as shortage of gas supply in Avdiivka and Pikuzy. On 23 June, an SMM unmanned aerial vehicle located an extensive leakage on the South Donbas water pipeline, putting at risk water supply for 1.5 million people on both sides of the contact line.

On 24 June, the SMM opened a new forward patrol base in Popasna, Luhansk region, allowing for sustained monitoring in the area.
