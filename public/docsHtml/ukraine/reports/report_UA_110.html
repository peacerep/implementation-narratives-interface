Latest News/Facts Matter  Between 14 and 27 June 2021, the SMM noted an increase in the number of ceasefire violations – 4,726, compared with 1,628 recorded in the previous two-week period.

 The Mission continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, and recorded 82 ceasefire violations inside the latter area.



Over the past two weeks, the SMM corroborated reports of two civilians injured due to detonations of explosive objects in non-government-controlled areas of Luhansk region.

This brings the total number of civilian casualties that have been confirmed since the beginning of 2021 to 39 (11 fatalities and 28 injuries).

 The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 28 times, 26 of which in non-government-controlled areas.

SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) were as-sessed as targeted by small-arms fire five times in non-government-controlled areas.

During the reporting period, the Mission’s UAVs experienced signal inter-ference assessed as caused by probable jamming or jamming on 109 occasions on both sides of the contact line, including ten instances when long-range UAVs were affected.  During the reporting period, the SMM continued to facilitate and monitor adher-ence to localized ceasefires to enable maintenance and operation of critical civil-ian infrastructure, including the operation of the pumping station near Vasylivka that provides water to about 1,200,000 civilians on both sides of the contact line.

 The Mission continued to observe civilians while crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line and their challenges.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska (open daily) and Novo-troitske/Olenivka (open twice a week, as the armed formations’ checkpoint there is only open on Mondays and Fridays).
