FACTS MATTER 

Between 20 May and 2 June, the SMM recorded almost 11,000 ceasefire violations (around 14,000 in the previous two-week period);

and observed 60 weapons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines.



The Mission also recorded almost 70 instances in which weapons that should have been withdrawn were used (mostly mortars and artillery in the vicinity of Svitlodarsk and Popasna).



In the past two weeks, the SMM confirmed that five civilians were injured.

In Horlivka, a woman sustained shrapnel injuries.

Her five-year-old granddaughter was admitted to hospital with incident-related shock.



Near the border with the Russian Federation in non-government-controlled areas of Luhansk, where there are no border crossing facilities, an SMM long-range UAV spotted trucks travelling west on a dirt track on two occasions.



In areas close to the contact line, the SMM continued to note humanitarian problems, now exacerbated by high summer temperatures.

In several settlements, including Oboz- ne, Khrystove, Svitle, Zoria, and Petrivka, residents told the SMM about a shortage or absence of running water. 

The SMM continued to enable repairs to infrastructure on both sides of the contact line, including the pumping station near Artema, a gas pipeline in Vesela Hora, the phenol sludge reservoir near Zalizne, a gas distribution station near Sakhanka, and power lines in Zaitseve.



The Mission monitored adherence to the ceasefire during a transfer of persons convict- ed before the conflict from non-government-controlled areas, as well as facilitated and monitored a transfer of human remains across the contact line of a member of the Ukrainian Armed Forces and a civilian.
