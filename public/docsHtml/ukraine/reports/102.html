Between 24 December 2018 and 13 January 2019, the Mission recorded 10,919 ceasefire violations.

Following the recommitment to the ceasefire on 29 December 2018, during the New Year and Christmas festivities, the Mission noted an initial decrease in the number of ceasefire violations.

After almost two weeks, the number of recorded ceasefire violations returned to the aver- age observed before the recommitment. On 13 January over 760 ceasefire violations were recorded by the Mission.

The SMM confirmed that 13 civilians were injured; 10 prior to the reporting period. On 10 January 2019, three employees of Voda Donbassa water company were injured when their vehicle was hit by a projectile while clearing snow from a road near the First Pumping Sta- tion in Vasylivka.

The Mission recorded 147 weapons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines (112 in areas not controlled by the Government). It also noted hardware in or close to residential areas, placing civilians in danger. For example, an armoured reconnaissance vehicle was observed about 30-40m from a house near Popasna and five armoured combat vehicles in Luhansk city.

In 2018 the SMM observed about 3,800 weapons in violation of withdrawal lines; about six per cent fewer than in 2017. About 60 per cent of such weapons were observed in non- government-controlled areas.

The Mission facilitated and monitored adherence to the ceasefire to enable assessments and repairs to 69 civilian infrastructure objects, including the Petrivske water pumping station near government-controlled Artema which provides approximately 150,000 people with water in mostly non-government-controlled areas.

In 2018 the Mission deployed 28,000 patrols to monitor localized adherence to the cease- fire, allowing for about 100 infrastructure repair works to be carried out, providing elec- tricity, water, gas and mobile communication to millions of civilians on both sides of the contact line.
