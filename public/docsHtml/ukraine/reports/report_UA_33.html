LATEST NEWS  Starting from 15 August, the SMM observed an increase in the level of violence, in particular in the Avdiivka-Yasynuvata-Donetsk airport triangle and around Stanytsia Luhanska.

 The level of violence remains high in other hotspots of Donetsk region:

the western and northern outskirts of Horlivka, areas around Shyrokyne and Pikuzy (formerly Kominternove), along the Svitlodarsk-Debaltseve road, as well as around Shchastia, Lobacheve and in western parts of Luhansk region.



The OSCE SMM Chief Monitor, Ertugrul Apakan, welcomed the decision by the Trilateral Contact Group (TCG) to renew commitment to adherence to the ceasefire at the start of the new school year and urged the sides to establish a full and comprehensive ceasefire.

The SMM continued to observe numerous weapons missing from storage sites as well as the presence of weapons in violation of withdrawal lines, often in and around residential areas.

Consequently, civilians and civilian infrastructure – including homes and utilities – are inadvertently caught in the crossfire.

 SMM monitors continue to be confronted by freedom-of-movement restrictions, particularly in areas not controlled by the Government.

On 20 August the SMM came under small-arms fire in Novooleksandrivka (www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/260541).
