Latest News/Facts Matter 

The OSCE Special Monitoring Mission (SMM) to Ukraine observed increased violence, in particular in the Donetsk region.

More than half of all ceasefire violations registered in Donetsk region were recorded in the Yasynuvata-Avdiivka area.

Significant fighting was also observed in Svitlodarsk, areas north-west of Donetsk city and at Donetsk airport.

The SMM also noted a rise in ceasefire violations in southern Donetsk region.



The situation in Luhansk region remained relatively calm with the exception of a spike in violence on 31 March–1 April in the areas of Pervomaisk and Stakhanov.



The SMM continued to note the presence and use of proscribed weapons within the security zone.



The presence of unexploded ordnance and mines remains a problem.

The deadline agreed in Minsk for completing the marking, fencing off and mapping of areas close to entry-exit civilian checkpoints along the contact line expired on 31 March.

So far the SMM has seen no systematic efforts by the sides to complete these actions.



On 31 March, the SMM monitored the situation affecting stranded civilians at checkpoints in Zolote.

Ukrainian Armed Forces had earlier announced the opening of a new checkpoint enabling civilians to cross the contact line but armed “LPR” members, citing security concerns, denied them passage.

This left over 300 civilians stranded on the road between the two checkpoints.

The SMM liaised with Ukrainian authorities to transport the civilians, many of them elderly, back to government-controlled areas.
