Facts Matter 

Between 6 and 19 August, the SMM noted continued violence along the contact line, in particular in areas east and north-east of Mariupol.

In all, the Mission recorded over 12,500 ceasefire violations.



The SMM continued to observe weapons in violation of agreed withdrawal lines (155 over the two-week period (61 in areas not controlled by the Government)).

It also noted large amounts of hardware being routed through government-controlled Kostiantynivka, and convoys of trucks entering and exiting Ukraine via a dirt track where there are no border crossing facilities in a non-government-controlled area of Donetsk region.



In addition to the ongoing threats posed by the presence of mines and unexploded ordnance – which the sides are obliged to remove – SMM monitors faced other threats, including one incident involving shots being fired and explosions occurring in their vicinity (Spot Report, www.osce.org/specialmonitoring-mission-to-ukraine/390878).

In another incident, a member of the armed formations, having first disengaged the safety of his assault rifle, pointed the weapon at SMM monitors (Spot Report, www.osce.org/specialmonitoring-mission-to-ukraine/390287).



On 19 August the SMM monitored the “Odessa Pride March” organized by the lesbian, gay, bi-sexual and transgender (LGBT) community in Odessa.

The Mission is following up on a number of alleged hate crimes that occurred during the reporting period involving members of the LGBT community in Lviv and Mariupol.
