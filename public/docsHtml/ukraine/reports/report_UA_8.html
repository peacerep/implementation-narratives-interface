Latest News/Facts Matter  Between 8 and 21 February 2021, the SMM recorded 2,048 ceasefire violations, compared with 1,697 in the previous two-week period.

For the 17th consecutive week, the SMM recorded no ceasefire violations attributable to the use of weap-ons that should have been withdrawn in accordance with the Minsk agreements.

 The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, observing people in the latter two areas during daytime.

On 16 February, an undetermined explosion occurred 300-400m from an SMM patrol close to the disengagement area near Petrivske.  During the two-week reporting period, the SMM corroborated no civilian casual-ties;

it has recorded only one injury since the beginning of 2021 (on 3 January).  The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized cease-fires to enable repairs to and maintenance and operation of critical civilian infra-structure on both sides of the contact line, including repairs to water infrastruc-ture between Maiorsk and Horlivka (providing water to about 120 people) and repairs of power lines in Vesela Hora and Obozne (providing electricity to more than 1,400 people).

 The SMM continued to observe civilians facing challenges when crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line in both directions.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use continued to be near Stanytsia Luhanska (operational daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (twice a week as the armed formations’ checkpoint there is only operational on Mondays and Fri-days).

 The Mission’s freedom of movement on the ground was restricted 19 times, all in non-government-controlled areas.

SMM unmanned aerial vehicles were tar-geted once by small-arms fire, in a non-government-controlled area;

and experi-enced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming on 23 occa-sions while flying on both sides of the contact line.
