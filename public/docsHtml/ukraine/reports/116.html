Areas in and around Donetsk airport were the primary focus of fighting. The situation in the Luhansk region remained tense with sporadic violence observed by the SMM in Shchastia, Trokhizbenka, Zolote and Stanytsia Luhanska.

Despite claims to the contrary, neither side has completed the withdrawal of heavy weapons. SMM unmanned aerial vehicles and monitors on the ground spotted tanks and firing positions on both sides of the contact line.

On 5 June the SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug briefed the United Nations Security Council on the recent situation in Ukraine.

On 9 June the SMM visited the area of Shyrokyne (20km east of Mariupol) for the first time since 14 May. The SMM observed evidence of recent shelling, including damage to civilian houses, the smell of smoke and burnt houses. Among the “DPR” members at the checkpoint at the north-eastern entrance to Shyrokyne, the SMM saw young female and male armed persons.

On 16 June in Minsk the SMM Chief Monitor Ambassador Ertugrul Apakan and Deputy Chief Monitor Hug attended another round of meetings coordinating the work of the working group on security issues under the auspices of the Trilateral Contact Group.
