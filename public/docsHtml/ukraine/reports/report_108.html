Latest News/Facts Matter 

Between 1 and 14 June, the SMM recorded a 30-per-cent reduction in the number of ceasefire violations (about 6,850, including about 2,750 explosions compared with about 9,850 and about 2,650, respectively, in the previous two-week period). 

The Mission continued monitoring the security situation in and around the three disen-gagement areas.

The SMM observed a calm situation inside the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska and Zolote, but recorded ceasefire violations inside the one near Petrivske.

Its ability to record ceasefire violations in the disengagement area near Petrivske was further limited when one of its cameras in the area was destroyed by small-arms fire on 2 June (see Spot Report 29/2020).

The Mission observed ongoing fortification work and/or trench extensions near all three disengagement areas, and the presence of people during evening and night hours near former positions inside the disengagement areas near Petrivske and Zolote. 

The Mission corroborated reports of three civilian casualties, all injuries, bringing the total since the beginning of 2020 to 51 (six fatalities and 45 injuries).



On 13 and 14 June, the SMM observed arranged civilian crossings in both directions through the entry-exit checkpoint near Stanytsia Luhanska and the corresponding check-point of the armed formations. 

The SMM continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to ena-ble repairs to, and construction and maintenance of, critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line.

This included maintenance of the phenol sludge reservoir near Zalizne and repairs to electricity infrastructure near Obozne, Zolote-5/Mykhailivka, Pervomaisk, Olenivka and Donetsk city’s Petrovskyi district. 

During the reporting period, the SMM observed over 870 anti-tank mines for the first time, including about 300 in fields near residential houses in Vodiane and about 440 (10 of which were near residential houses) in fields near Shyroka Balka.



Members of the armed formations continued to deny the SMM passage at checkpoints along official crossing routes in Donetsk and Luhansk regions (see Spot Report 30/2020 and Spot Report 31/2020).

Overall, the Mission’s freedom of movement on the ground was restricted 44 times (all in areas not controlled by the Government).

SMM un-manned aerial vehicles (UAV) were subjected to signal interference on both sides of the contact line on 28 occasions, and were targeted by small-arms fire six times (four times in non-government-controlled areas).
