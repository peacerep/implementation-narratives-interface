Between 14 and 27 October, the SMM recorded about 16,200 ceasefire violations, in- cluding about 3,500 explosions. This compares with about 17,000 ceasefire violations, including about 3,200 explosions, in the previous two-week period.

The Mission confirmed reports of 14 civilian casualties, all injuries, including five boys and two girls injured by mines and unexploded ordnance (UXO). Children, especially boys, are often drawn to mines and UXO, mistaking them for toys or objects of value. Since the beginning of 2019, the SMM has confirmed 137 civilian casualties (16 killed, 121 injured).

The SMM saw damage caused by small-arms fire to two functioning schools in Oleksan- drivka and Zolote. Since the beginning of the year, the SMM has observed damage to 14 operational educational facilities, including 11 schools and three kindergartens, on both sides of the contact line due to shelling and small-arms fire. Other civilian properties in settlements located along the contact line also continue to be exposed, including in Dokuchaievsk, where in 2019, the SMM has seen damage from shelling and gunfire to 50 civilian properties.

The SMM continued facilitating the repair and maintenance of essential civilian infra- structure on both sides of the contact line, including repairs ensuring the supply of wa- ter to more than 150,000 civilians.

The SMM recorded no ceasefire violations inside or within the 5km periphery of the Stanytsia Luhanska disengagement area. Inside the area, the Mission continued to ob- serve workers from government-controlled areas repairing the broken section of the Stanytsia Luhanska bridge.

On 29 October (outside the reporting period), the SMM saw that disengagement from the Zolote disengagement area had begun. Additional SMM patrols, assisted by static cameras and unmanned aerial vehicles, observed the launch of flares by the Ukrainian Armed Forces and the armed formations, signalling both sidesâ€™ readiness to disengage, and subsequently Ukrainian Armed Forces personnel and members of the armed for- mations exiting the disengagement area, together with their weapons.

On 25 October, in Kyiv, Kharkiv, Odessa and Zaporizhzhia, the SMM observed peaceful gatherings of about 80-150 people (mostly men), expressing messages critical of the recent developments concerning certain areas of Donetsk and Luhansk regions.
