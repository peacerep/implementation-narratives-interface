Between 28 June and 11 July 2021, the SMM noted a decrease in the number of ceasefire violations – 3,975, compared with 4,726 recorded in the previous two-week period. In an address to the OSCE Permanent Council, Ambassador Y. Halit Çevik, Chief Moni-tor of the SMM, stressed that the security situation in eastern Ukraine is not improv-ing and challenges are becoming acute, calling on the sides to return to a construc-tive approach.

The Mission continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, again recording ceasefire violations inside the latter area.

Over the past two weeks, the SMM corroborated reports of seven civilian casualties, including a civilian who died due to an explosion in Donetsk city. This brings the total number of civilian casualties that have been confirmed since the beginning of 2021 to 46 (12 fatalities and 34 injuries).

The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 36 times, 34 of which were in non-government-controlled areas. SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) were as-sessed as targeted by small-arms fire twice – once in government-controlled and once in non-government-controlled areas. The Mission’s UAVs experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming or jamming on 114 occasions on both sides of the contact line, including one instance when a long-range UAV was lost due to dual GPS signal interference assessed as jamming (see Spot Report 16/2021).

The SMM camera at the Oktiabr mine near non-government-controlled Donetsk city was damaged due to small-arms fire (see Spot Report 17/2021).

During the reporting period, the SMM continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to enable the maintenance and operation of critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line, including the maintenance of power lines near Olenivka (providing electricity to a couple thousand civilians) and repairs to a water pipeline near Berezove (providing water to about 8,000 civilians).

The Mission continued to observe civilians while crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line and their challenges. It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska (open daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (open twice a week, as the corresponding checkpoint of the armed for-mations there is only open on Mondays and Fridays).
