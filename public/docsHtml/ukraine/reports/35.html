The recommitment to the ceasefire on 30 March has yielded insignificant results, with a 7 percent decrease in the number of ceasefire violations (9,300) recorded by the OSCE SMM between 2 and 15 April, compared with the previous reporting period. Violence increased notably during the second week of this reporting period, particularly in and around Staromykhailivka and in the Kuibyshevskyi district of Donetsk city on 10 and 11 April, resulting in the injury of at least five civilians. The Chief Monitor, along with the OSCE Project Co-ordinator in Ukraine Vaidotas Verba, in marking International Mine Awareness Day on 4 April, also called on the sides to fulfil their mine action commitments. Their failure to do so has resulted in 11 civilian deaths and 18 civilian injuries so far this year. (See: www.osce.org/project-coordinator-in-ukraine/376852).

Mines and unexploded ordnance continued to impede SMM patrols. Members of the armed formations in Luhansk region also continued to restrict SMM access to border areas near the Russian Federation not controlled by the Government. For the ninth consecutive week, SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) continued to be subject to small-arms fire and the recently re-launched long-range SMM UAVs were subject to jamming on a number of occasions. Of particular concern was an incident on 5 April, when a member of an armed formation, with a charged assault rifle, threatened SMM monitoring officers. (See: www.osce.org/special- monitoring-mission-to-ukraine/377194).
