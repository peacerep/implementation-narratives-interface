Between 28 January and 10 February, the SMM recorded over 10,400 ceasefire violations (compared with almost 9,500 in the previous two-week period); and observed 130 weap-ons in violation of the agreed withdrawal lines (53 in areas not controlled by the Govern-ment). In a number of incidents, civilians were directly impacted. School children in Zolote were sent home following shelling in the area; workers preparing for a funeral in Cher-malyk came under small-arms fire and required SMM facilitation before reaching safety; and a truck driver delivering humanitarian aid near Kadiivka was subjected to small-arms fire.

The SMM corroborated the deaths of two elderly people attempting to cross the contact line, taking the number of cases to 14 since late December 2018. The Mission continued to observe long queues of civilians at entry-exit checkpoints. Separately, the Ukrainian State Border Guard Service told the SMM that about 37,000 crossings of the contact line had been made by civilians every day in 2018, amounting to over 13 million crossings throughout the year.

The Mission facilitated and monitored localized adherence to the ceasefire on 47 occa-sions to enable repairs to 15 civilian infrastructure facilities along the contact line and continued to facilitate the operation of the Donetsk Filtration Station. Within the re-porting period, repair work was completed to a water pipeline near Berezove, which pro-vides potable water to around 10,000 civilians on both sides of the contact line, and pow-er lines in Katerynivka, allowing for the restoration of electricity to about 500 residents.

Following up on developments related to the Azov Sea, the SMM heard from numerous interlocutors who highlighted negative implications, including the suspension of fishing and the threat of lay-offs in the fishing industry; the loss of contracts for ship repair yards and increased insurance costs for companies shipping cargo.
