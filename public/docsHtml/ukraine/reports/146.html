On 20 November, construction work at the broken section of Stanytsia Luhanska bridge was completed and the newly-constructed span of the bridge opened for civilians cross- ing the contact line.

The SMM continued monitoring the security situation inside the three disengagement areas, noting in particular demining activities in the Petrivske and Zolote disengagement areas.

Between 11 and 24 November, the SMM recorded about 12,900 ceasefire violations, including about 3,300 explosions. This compares with 10,500 ceasefire violations, includ- ing about 2,400 explosions, in the previous two-week period.

The SMM recorded 35 weapons in violation of their respective withdrawal lines, com- pared to 138 in the previous two-week period. The Mission continued to observe the presence of heavy weapons, as well as military and military-type positions and hard- ware, in and near residential areas, including a self-propelled anti-aircraft gun in Ku- rakhove and a tank in Novohryhorivka.

The SMM continued to observe the presence of anti-tank mines, many in areas where civilians cross the contact line, including near Pyshchevyk and Olenivka.

The Mission confirmed one civilian casualty: a man injured by gunfire reportedly while walking in Donetsk city’s Petrovskyi district on 13 November. Since the beginning of 2019, the SMM has confirmed 144 civilian casualties (18 killed, 126 injured).

The SMM continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to the ceasefire to enable re- pairs and maintenance to critical civilian infrastructure, including repairs to pipelines between Zolote-3/Stakhanovets and Popasna (providing potable water to 300,000 civil- ians), and power lines in Spartak (supplying electricity to 100 local residents).

The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 53 times, all but two in non- government-controlled areas. In addition, SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) contin- ued to be subjected to signal interference on a regular basis, and were targeted by small -arms fire on one occasion.
