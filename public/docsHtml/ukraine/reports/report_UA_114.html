FACTS MATTER 

Between 3 and 16 May 2021, the SMM noted an increase in the number of ceasefire viola- tions – recording 4,848 of them, compared with 3,135 in the previous two-week period.



The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, recording increased kinetic activity inside or near the latter two areas and noting the presence of people inside the latter two areas.



Over the past two weeks, the SMM corroborated eight civilian casualties, including two fatalities.

This brings the total number of civilian casualties that occurred since the begin- ning of 2021 to 24 (nine fatalities and 15 injuries).



The Mission saw damage to 14 private properties and civilian infrastructure sites on both sides of the contact line, including to a functional hospital in a government-controlled area of Donetsk region and a functional school in a non-government-controlled area of Luhansk region.

Since the beginning of the year, the Mission confirmed damage to 30 private properties and civilian infrastructure sites.



On both sides of the contact line in Donetsk and Luhansk regions, SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) continued spotting mines, including 4,165 anti-tank mines, about 100 probable anti-personnel mines and three pieces of unexploded ordnance.



The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 13 times, all in non-government- controlled areas.

SMM UAVs were targeted six times by small-arms fire on both sides of the contact line.

The Mission’s UAVs experienced signal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming or jamming on 105 occasions, including 34 instances when long- range UAVs were affected (forcing one emergency landing, see Spot Report 13/2021).



The SMM continued to observe civilians while crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line and their challenges.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska (opened daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (twice a week, as the armed formations’ checkpoint there is only open on Mondays and Fridays).



The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to ena- ble maintenance and operation of critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the con- tact line, including maintenance of water infrastructure near Novotoshkivske and near Holmivskyi, altogether providing water to around 9,000 civilians.
