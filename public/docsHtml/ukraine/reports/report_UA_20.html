FACTS MATTER 

Between 13 and 26 January, the SMM recorded about 8,800 ceasefire violations, com- pared to about 5,300 in the previous two-week period.



The SMM recorded 73 weapons in violation of their respective withdrawal lines, com- pared to 51 in the previous two-week period:

11 in government-controlled areas and 62 in areas outside government control.



The SMM continued monitoring the security situation in and around the three disen- gagement areas.

Inside the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska and Zolote, it observed a calm situation.

It recorded ceasefire violations and saw persons in camou- flage clothing inside the Petrivske disengagement area.

In the second week of the re- porting period, members of the armed formations hindered the Mission’s monitoring of the Petrivske disengagement area, restricting access to Petrivske and the nearby SMM camera site on six consecutive days.

SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) experienced GPS signal interference in the same vicinity.



The SMM continued to observe anti-tank mines and unexploded ordnance, in particular in southern Donetsk region on both sides of the contact line.

Specifically, SMM UAVs spotted 350 anti-tank mines south of Pyshchevyk, 250 east of Vodiane, about 65 in east- ern areas of Hnutove, and 365 in areas north and south-east of Pikuzy.

The UAVs also spotted 130 anti-tank mines placed near and across the road between Talakivka and Pikuzy.



The Mission corroborated reports of seven civilian casualties from the detonation of explosive devices and mines, including one fatality – a 35-year old man who died when a grenade he had reportedly found while collecting scrap metal exploded in Shevchenko in Donetsk region on 11 January.



The Mission confirmed that two elderly men had died due to natural causes while waiting to cross the contact line, bringing the number of such cases since the beginning of the year to four. 

The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted 56 times, all in non-government- controlled areas.



SMM UAVs were subjected to signal interference on both sides of the contact line on 42 occasions, and were targeted by small-arms fire six times in non-government-controlled areas.
