Between 11 and 22 December 2017, the SMM OSCE recorded over 26,000 ceasefire violations - a 25 percent increase compared to the previous two weeks. On 11 December the SMM recorded over 3,500 ceasefire violations alone.

Following the recommitment to cease fire from 00:00 on 23 December the SMM recorded 328 ceasefire violations throughout the first 24 hours. On 24 December the Mission recorded a significant decrease – 64 ceasefire violations.

From 11 to 24 December the violence resulted in a number of civilian casualties, with one person killed and 20 injured. This brings the total number of civilian casualties confirmed by the SMM from the beginning of this year as of 24 December 2017 to 470, including 85 fatalities.

From 11 to 17 December, the Mission monitored and facilitated 30 “windows of silence” – localized adherence to the ceasefire – which allowed for inspection, repair and maintenance work on ten critical civilian infrastructure facilities on both sides of the contact line. On 14 December, the SMM facilitated and monitored repairs of essential infrastructure near Zalizne, Artema and Vesela Hora. However, following the withdrawal of the Russian Federation Armed Forces officers of the Joint Centre for Control and Co- ordination (JCCC) on 18 December, no such works were conducted between 18 and 24 December.

The majority of recorded ceasefire violations were concentrated in five known “hot-spots” along the contact line; the violence was particularly heavy in the areas of Avdiivka, Yasynuvata and Donetsk airport; areas south and south-east of Svitlodarsk and the Popasna -Pervomaisk area.
