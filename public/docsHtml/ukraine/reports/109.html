

OSCE Secretary General Zannier and SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan on 12 February met with the monitoring team based in Dnepropetrovsk and visited a Centre for displaced persons affected by the conflict .



On 13 February, Zannier and Apakan in a joint statement in Kyiv said that the SMM is ready to monitor the ceasefire, and to engage all sides that are entrusted to upholding the implementation of the peace agreements.



SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug visited eastern Ukraine on 16-17 February, including the headquarters of the Joint Centre for Control and Co-ordination (JCCC).

Negotiations focused on monitor’s access to Debaltseve.

SMM visited Debaltseve on 21 February and facilitated access for humanitarian aid.



The SMM is ready to monitor and verify the process of the withdrawal of heavy weaponry in eastern Ukraine.

In order to verify the withdrawal – to testify that specific weapons really have been and remain withdrawn – the SMM needs to know what heavy weapons the sides have, where these weapons are now, and where they will be located after they have been withdrawn.

The Chief Monitor sent a request for this information to the signatories of the Minsk agreements, but so far their responses have not been received.
