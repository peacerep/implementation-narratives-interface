On 3-6 July OSCE SMM Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug visited Mariupol, Donetsk and Luhansk, met the SMM teams working there, conducted meetings with Joint Centre for Control and Coordination representatives and other relevant interlocutors.

On 4 July Hug and other SMM monitors went to Shyrokyne. All civilians have left the village. Up to 80% of the civilian houses and infrastructure are damaged beyond repair. SMM did not observe any “DPR” presence but Ukrainian Armed Forces in south-west corner of village.

On 3-14 July the SMM brokered a ceasefire, paving the way for demining and repair works on a water pipeline near the line of contact, between Maiorsk and Horlivka. On 14 July workers were able to finalize repair works at the first pipeline.

On 8 July Alexander Hug visited Minsk, for the fifth round of negotiations of the Working Group on Security within the Trilateral Contact Group. It was a construc-tive meeting and SMM hopes that concrete agreements will be reached at the next meeting scheduled for 21 July.

On 12 July the SMM issued the Spot Report: Monitoring events in the wake of deadly shooting in Mukacheve: www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/171881

On 13 July, in Vienna, OSCE SMM Chief Monitor Ambassador Ertugrul Apakan addressed at the joint meeting of OSCE Permanent Council and Forum for Security Co-operation on the situation in Ukraine and activities carried out by the SMM in responding to the crisis.
