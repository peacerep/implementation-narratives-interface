Latest News/Facts Matter  Between 22 February and 7 March, the SMM noted a 54% reduction in the number of ceasefire violations, recording 944, compared with 2,048 in the previous two-week period.

 On 7 March, an SMM long-range unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) crashed near Romanivka, Donetsk region.

The aircraft had experienced dual GPS technical failure about an hour before crashing (see Spot Report 2/2021).  The SMM continued to monitor the disengagement areas near Stanytsia Luhanska, Zolote and Petrivske, observing people in the latter two areas dur-ing daytime.

Inside the area near Petrivske, it observed previously seen mines and recorded two ceasefire violations.  During the two-week reporting period, the SMM corroborated no civilian casualties;

it has recorded only one injury that occurred since the beginning of 2021 (on 3 January).

 The Mission continued to facilitate and monitor adherence to localized ceasefires to enable repairs to and maintenance and operation of critical civilian infrastructure on both sides of the contact line, including repairs to power lines near Shchastia, Stepanivka and Almazna, altogether providing electricity for several hundred thousand civilians.

 The SMM continued to observe civilians facing challenges when crossing, or attempting to cross, the contact line.

It again noted that the only crossing points civilians were able to use were near Stanytsia Luhanska (operational daily) and Novotroitske/Olenivka (twice a week as the armed formations’ checkpoint there is only operational on Mondays and Fridays).  The Mission’s freedom of movement on the ground was restricted nine times, all in non-government-controlled areas.

SMM UAVs experienced sig-nal interference assessed as caused by probable jamming on 35 occasions while flying on both sides of the contact line.
