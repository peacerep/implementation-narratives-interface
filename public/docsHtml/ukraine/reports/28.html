LATEST NEWS 

A high level of violence around Svitlodarsk and in areas to the north and west of Donetsk city, in particular around Avdiivka and Yasynuvata, was noted by the OSCE Special Monitoring Mission to Ukraine (SMM) over the past two weeks.



On 17 March the SMM facilitated and monitored the resumption of operations at Yasynuvata water filtration plant which had been previously suspended due to violence.



The situation in Luhansk region remained relatively calm.



The SMM noted the presence of proscribed weapons within the security zone as well as many others missing from permanent storage sites.



The SMM’s freedom of movement remained problematic, mostly in areas not controlled by the Government, particularly near the border with Russian Federation and north-east of Mariupol. 

On 17-22 March the OSCE SMM Principal Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug visited eastern Ukraine.

Hug patrolled Donetsk and Luhansk regions, met with SMM teams, and conducted meetings with Joint Centre for Control and Co-operation representatives and other relevant interlocutors.
