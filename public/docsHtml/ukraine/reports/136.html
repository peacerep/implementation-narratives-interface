Between 8 and 21 January 2018, the OSCE SMM recorded around 13,000 ceasefire violations – a 55 per cent increase compared with the previous two-week reporting period.

The majority of ceasefire violations were recorded in areas east and north-east of Mariupol; in the western and northern outskirts of Horlivka; in areas south-east and east of Svitlodarsk; and in the Avdiivka-Yasynuvata-Donetsk Airport area. The latter two areas were the most affected, with 72 percent of all ceasefire violations recorded there during the reporting period.

Armed violence between 8 and 21 January resulted in civilian casualties, leaving three people killed and two injured.

Between 8 and 21 January, the Mission facilitated and monitored six ‘windows of silence’ – localized adherence to the ceasefire – which allowed for inspection, repair and maintenance work on three critical civilian infrastructure facilities on both sides of the contact line: one near Shchastia on 19 January notably resulted in the restoration of all mobile telecommunications services across the contact line and within non-government-controlled areas of Luhansk region. Disruption to services – also affecting Donetsk region – had started on 11 January. Services remain severed across the contact line in Donetsk region and within non-government-controlled areas of the region, where security guarantees have not yet been provided to enable repairs to the damaged link in Olenivka.
