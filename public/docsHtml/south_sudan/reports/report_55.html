EXECUTIVE SUMMARY During October and November 2021 in Yambio County, SPLM/A-IO military forces conducted what was termed a ‘mobilisation exercise’ to round up SPLM/A-IO deserters.

During the course of the activity forced recruitment took place after which civilians were taken to the SPLM/A-IO camp at Li-Rangu and onwards to the Maridi Training Centre.

In the course of the recruitment a young girl was taken to Li -Rangu SPLM/A-IO camp and forced to become a ‘wife’ of an officer.

SPLM/A-IO took money from the families of the recruited civilians in order to facilitate their release.

Amongst the individuals recruited by the SPLM/A-IO were child soldiers.

It is the conclusion of CTSAMVM that:

By recruiting civilians in the Yambio County area in October and November 2021, the SPLM/A-IO acted in violation of the R-ARCSS, specifically article 2.1.8.

By abducting civilians and extorting money for their release the SPLM/A-IO acted in violation of the R-ARCSS, specifically 2.2.10. 7.0.

By abducting and placing a vulnerable person, a girl, into the hands of SPLM/A-IO officers the SPLM/A-IO acted in violation of the R-ARCSS, specifically 2.0.10.0.

By recruiting civilians under the age of 18 the SPLM/A-lO acted in violation of the R-ARCSS, specifically 2.0.10.0.

Throughout the investigation the SPLM/A-IO provided complete access to CTSAMVM and co-operated in every possible way.

The co-operation demonstrated by SPLM/A-IO Div 9B should be used as a model for all parties when CTSAMVM conduct investigations of serious allegations.

% RECRUITMENT IN WESTERN EQUATORIA STATE Introduction 1.

Background:

a. SPLM/A-IO forces in Western Equatoria State (WES) are under SPLM/A-IO Sector 6.

The Sector HQ is situated in Li-Rangu Payam, Yambio County, about 23 km north of Yambio town.

The Sector Commander is Lt Gen Alfred Futuyo who is also the Governor of Western Equatoria State and deputised by Maj Gen John Martin Sunday.

b. SPLM/A-IO Division 9B also has its HQ in Li-Rangu Payam.

It is commanded by Maj Gen Simon Samuel Ali.

c. In October 2021 the County Commissioner of Yambio County was informed by the SPLM/A-IO that they intended to collect soldiers who had “deserted” Cantonment Sites and were living amongst the civilian population.

This move was welcomed by the County Commissioner because some of these SPLM/A- soldiers were allegedly becoming involved in criminal activities and causing problems.

2.

Allegations:

a. On November 2021 the Deputy Governor of Western Equatoria State, Dr Kennedy Goaniko, informed CTSAMVM that his office had received several complaints of abductions and forced recruiting of youths by SPLM/A-IO Division 9B in Li-Rangu.

He said that the Yambio County Commissioner could provide more details.

b. Later on the same day — November 2021 — CTSAMVM was approached by a civilian from a village Km north of Yambio who alleged that on 27 October 2021 his 17year-old nephew had been forcefully recruited by soldiers he suspected to fy be SPLM/A-IO from Li-Rangu.

He went on to say that his nephew called him on November and declared he was in Maridi Training Centre. 3.

Findings 5.

Local authorities and civil society organisations:

a. On November 2021 CTSAMVM met with the Yambio County Commissioner, who confirmed that in October 2021 his office had been notified by the SPLM/A-IO that they would be “mobilising” their forces who had “deserted” Cantonment Sites and bases.

The County Commissioner said this move was welcomed by the local authorities, because these soldiers had been causing problems in the communities.

However, the County Commissioner reported that he soon began to receive complaints from Boma chiefs and village headmen of forceful recruitment by the SPLM/A-IO.

He said that people lived in fear of the SPLM/A-IO, and that the majority of youths had fled their villages to take refuge in towns to avoid being abducted.

The County :

Wy Commissioner provided CTSAMVM with a list of youths allegedly abducted by the SPLM/A-lO. b. On 22 November 2021 CTSAMVM met with the Head of Governance Technical Committee of the Western Equatoria State Network for Civil Society Organisations.

He repeated what had been said by the County Commissioner, and said Civil Society organisations had received reports of forceful recruitment, particularly in the areas of Saura, Bazungua, Ikpiro, Ndovuru and Akrgbodi Bomas.

c. On 23 November 2021 CTSAMVM met with one of the Yambio Municipality Chiefs, who corroborated the information given by the County Commissioner.

CTSAMVM also met with the Village Headman at Akorogbodi, who reported that in mid-November 2021 seven (07) youths from Tindoka village ran to his house to take refuge because they were being pursued by SPLM/A-IO soldiers who “wanted to recruit them”.

The Headman said he had received numerous reports of abductions and forceful recruitment by the SPLM/A-IO in the local area.

d. CTSAMVM then met with the Youth Leader and Village Headman at Tindoka Village.

They reported that in about mid-November 2021 SPLM/A-IO soldiers raided the village and “arrested” 16 youths including several children, two of whom were only years old.

These youth were taken to Li-Rangu and their families asked to pay money for their release.

The Headman said that “quite a number” had been released following payment.

The Youth Leader and Headman said that they were expecting another raid, and as a result all the youths had left the village to hide in the bush.

CTSAMVM observed no youths in the village.

•. CTSAMVM met with the village Youth Leader and a local policeman at Nangbangi village.

The Youth Leader's brothers had been “arrested” by the SPLM/A-IO but had managed to escape.

Both the Youth Leader and the policemen related reports of abduction and forceful recruitment by the so SPLM/A-IO, and that the youths in the area have been traumatised as a result.

Assessment 13.

Forcible recruitment:

The mobilisation conducted by SPLM/A-IO Division 6B of Sector based in Ri-Rangu was intended to round up SPLM/A-IO deserters.

However, the activity also resulted in forcible recruitment in the Yambio County 12 M area in October and November 2021.

This is evidenced by victim statements and by information given by citizens and local authorities/traditional leaders.

Further evidence is provided by the Acting Chief Instructor of Maridi Training Centre who Said he had to release two members of fire and wildlife services who had been “abducted on the way

[to the Training Centre]”.

In addition, CTSAMVM identified two other individuals who were recruited but subsequently released, one of whom was released after his family paid money to SPLM/A-IO Div 9B. 14.

Scale of Recruitment.

Allegations were made that there was large scale forced recruitment underway in Yambio County.

CTSAMVM is not able to verify the scale of recruitment, there are conflicting accounts on the numbers involved.

The statement made by the Deputy Governor at the State Security Committee meeting that 60 forcefully recruited civilians had been taken to Li-Rangu could not be substantiated.

However CTSAMVM conducted extensive engagements with Boma chiefs, village headmen, and youth leaders and it is assessed that the recruitment was greater than the Specified cases outlined in Para 13. 15.

Duration of recruitment:

The “mobilisation exercise” carried out by the SPLM/A-IO did not finish at the end of October as claimed by the commander of SPLM/A-IO Division 9B. This is evidenced by victim and witness statements, and also by the fact that the release letters held by CTSAMVM are dated 20 and 22 November. 16.

Extortion:

The SPLM/A-IO has been extorting money from civilians abducted in Yambio County.

This is evidenced by statements made by witnesses and victims, and also by the statement by Maj Gen Simon Samuel Ali Solomon, who admitted that “some of his officers might have extorted money from victim’s families”.

17.Fear:

People in Yambio County are scared of being forcibly recruited.

This is evidenced not only by statements recorded by CTSAMVM, but also CTSAMVM's observations on the ground.

18.

SGBV:

The case of the young girl allegedly forcibly taken to and held at Ri-Rangu (paragraphs 7a) is a strong indicator of SGBV, in particular forced marriage.

The evidence for this is that she clearly said that she had been given as a “wife” to an SPLM/A-IO officer.

It is of concern that, according to her statement, she was abducted along with two other girls.

The case of the 15 year-old girl at Maridi Training Centre (paragraph 8) is also of concern.

19.

Underage trainees at Maridi Training Centre:

The Acting Chief Instructor at Maridi Training Centre freely admitted that of the SPLM/A-IO trainees were 17 years old.

Conclusions 20.CTSAMVM concludes that a. By forcefully recruiting civilians, including minors, in the Yambio County area in October and November 2021, the SPLM/A-IO acted in violation of the R- ARCSS, specifically articles 2.0.8, 2.1.10.0 and 2.0.10.0.

b.

By abducting civilians and extorting money for their release the SPLM/A-IO acted in violation of the R-ARCSS, specifically 2.0.10.

7.0. c. By abducting and placing a vulnerable female minor into the hands of an SPLM/A-lO officer in a form of forced marriage the SPLM/A-IO acted in violation of the R-ARCSS, specifically 2.0.10.0.

General:

It is apparent that an agreed exercise by the SPLM/A-IO to “mobilise” “deserters” in order to remove them from communities and return them to Cantonment Sites, turned into forced recruitment of civilians who had no military training and had not been in the SPLM/A-IO. 14 w) 22.

Recommendations:

CTSAMVM recommends that:

a. Action is taken by JDB to Stop recruitment throughout the country.

b. The SPLM/A-IO takes the necessary measures to instil discipline in Division 9b, and ensures that those guilty of recruitment are held accountable.

c. That the SPLM/A-IO investigates the case of the young girl allegedly given as a “wife” to an officer at Li-Rangu (paragraph 7a).

d. The WES security committee is congratulated for highlighting the concerns raised over recruitment and for its pro-active stance to stop the recruitment and mobilisation. €.
