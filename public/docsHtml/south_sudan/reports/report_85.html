SUMMARY

On 11 January 2021 Armed Youth from Koch Country conducted a cattle raid at Rubkona.

There were civilian casualties and large numbers of cattle were taken.

On February 2021 SSPDF with state officials went to Koch County to negotiate the release of the cattle.

On February 2021 the SSPDF and accompanying civilian party was attacked by Armed Youth at the village of Rier in Koch County.

SSPDF and civilians incurred casualties and weapons were taken from SSPDF soldiers.

On 20 February 2021 SSPDF conducted a patrol under the command of Colonel Tholow Wich SSPDF to retrieve Cattle and weapons from villages in Koch County.

The patrol was not successful in retrieving the cattle, but some weapons were recovered.

During the patrol on 20 February 2021 the SSPDF soldiers destroyed houses and looted from civilians in the villages of Logat, Jajah and Jaak in Koch County.

The actions of the SSPDF resulted in the displacement of civilians.

By conducting operations to destroy dwellings and displace civilians SSPDF has failed to protect civilians and is in violation of ACOH December 2017.

Background:

In the course of routine monitoring on 21 January CTSAMVM was informed that on 11 January 2021 armed youth from Koch County stole 3000 cattle from Nhialdu, There were also clashes in the same area on February and 20 February involving armed youth from Koch County and SSPDF.

CTSAMVM through the JMCO instructed MVT Bentiu to conduct an investigation to verify the circumstances of the reported incidents and to make an assessment 2.

Allegations a.

The Deputy Executive Officer of Rubkona County (Mr Dak M Kueygang) alleged that on 11 January 2021 that Armed Youth from Koch County raided Nhialdhu village resulting in the killing of people and wounding 11 as well as the seizure of 3000 cattle.

On 22 March 2021 CTSAMVM received information that on the night of 20 February 2021 SSPDF forces attacked the villages of Logat, Jajah and Jaak north of Koch town and destroyed dwellings and displaced civilians, It was alleged that the attacks were in retaliation for SSPDF casualties incurred on February when attacked by armed youth in Rier Village, Koch County. 3.

Findings 5.

On 11th January 2021 3000 head of cattle were raided in Nhialdiu and Rubkona.

It was reported that during the raid civilians were killed and others wounded.

The raid was conducted by armed youth from Koch County. 6.

After the raid the leadership of the state formed a committee comprised of the Koch County Commissioner Mr. Gordon Koang Biel, Member of Parliament Galwak Gatwesh and Lt Col Jagei (SSPDF).

The committee later visited Koch County to negotiate the return of the cattle but were unsuccessful. 7.

Commander Ground Forces, in Juba instructed " Division SSPDF to assist in the recovery of the stolen cattle.

On February 2021 35 SSPDF soldiers with representatives of the Rubkona County leadership proceeded to Koch County Rier Village, Koch County to make a second attempt to negotiate the release of the Stolen cattle.

During the negotiation, the SSPDF and civilians from Rubkona were :

surrounded by armed youth anda gunfight broke out.

It was reported that SSPDF were killed, and several weapons were taken from the soldiers.

SSPDF were unable to verify the exact number of weapons which were taken but PKM Machine guns, RPGs, pistols and an unknown number of AK47s were amongst those reported to have been taken.

Commander Division SSPDF received instructions from the Governor of Unity State to retrieve the seized weapons.

Colonel Tholow Wich was tasked to assemble a detachment and go to Koch County and retrieve the weapons peacefully without engaging in fighting.

In the event that the weapons could not be retrieved peacefully it is reported that Col Tholow Wich was ordered to return to HQ SSPDF Division for further orders. 9.

On 20 February 2021 SSPDF soldiers under the command of Col Tholow Wich moved by vehicle into Koch County.

The vehicle movement was observed by UN soldiers based in the county.

The UN Unit received subsequent reports of fighting.

On 21 February 2021 a UN Patrol visited the villages of Jahjah, Lolgal and Jah and observed there to be over 120 dwellings destroyed by fire and evidence of looting.

10.A resident of Jahjah village witnessed the fighting which took place on 20 February 2021.

When the soldiers arrived in the villages the armed youth had vacated the area.

Soldiers were reported to be firing into the bush in case the armed youth were present.

The soldiers then proceeded to set fire to dwellings and an NGO facility which provided Support to the community.

No Casualties were reported but there was extensive civilian displacement.

Reports that SSPDF soldiers came under fire from the armed youth during the incident were not substantiated.

fj | Assessment 12.

The seizure of 3000 cattle and the killing and wounding of civilians in Nhialdhu on 11 January 2021 was conducted by armed youth from Koch County.

The incident triggered a sequence of further violent clashes. 13.

State and county authorities tried to resolve the issues arising from the cattle raid in a peaceful manner, Unfortunately, the attempts to negotiate failed in the face of @ heavily armed response by the armed youth who, on the evidence provided to CTSAMVM, appear to have been the chief protagonists.

14.When Col Tholow Wich and his soldiers attempted to retrieve weapons from the villages of Logat, Jajah and Jaak in Koch County on 20 February 2021 the soldiers under his command destroyed houses, looted Property and destroyed an NGO facility resulting in the displacement of civilians.

There are grounds for considering that the violence was inflicted as a retaliation against the Koch community for the casualties inflicted on SSPDF soldiers on February 2021.

15.SSPDF have accepted responsibility for the action carried out by soldiers under the command of Col Tholow Wich.

Furthermore, SSPDF have reportedly investigated the incidents which took place on 20 February and the officer responsible has been arrested and is in Juba awaiting court martial Procedures, Conclusion 16.The effect of the destruction of houses and NGO facilities in Koch County by SSPDF was to cause widespread civilian displacement in violation of Article 2.0.10 of the R-ARCSS.

SSPDF has failed to fulfil its Obligations to protect civilians as | required by Articles , and of the Agreement on Cessation of Hostilies 21 ;

December 2017 as incorporated into Article 2.1.10 of the R-ARCSS.
