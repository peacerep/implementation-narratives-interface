| Introduction , Background a. Under article 2.0.8 of the R-ARCSS, the Parties agreed to “cease security forces recruitment and training”.

Any recruitment and training of new recruits would not only be in violation of article 2.1.8 but also those other provisions such as article 2.2.10.0, 2.0.10.0, 2.0.10.0.1, 2.0.10.0.4 among others. b. Since the signing of the ACoH, CTSAMVM has received many allegations of forced recruitment by the Parties.

It has always been a challenging issue to investigate.

The Parties are unwilling to cooperate and too often witnesses unwilling to talk to CTSAMVM for fear of reprisals.

c. During 2020 there were allegations of forced recruitment in Unity State, specifically in the Bentiu/Rubkona areas.

CTSAMVM _ undertook an investigation but was unable to find any evidence of forced recruitment in the mentioned areas.

SSPDF commanders told CTSAMVM that it was not recruitment but bringing back to units “deserters” or soldiers who had left SSPDF and returned to their villages. d. Between 2021 and 2022 CTSAMVM continued to receive allegations of recruitment in the Bentiu/Rubkona areas, and in particular in the IDP camps.

On 25 January 2022, the Bentiu IDF Youth Forum issued a press statement condemning “rampant” arrest of young people by the armed forces.

•. In September 2021 CTSAMVM received allegations from the SPLM/A-IO that the SSPDF was training “new recruits” in Digala.

CTSAMVM was unable to get access to Digala, and indeed on February 2022 was turned back at gunpoint (CTSAMVM Report 2022/06).

t SW | BE YY f. On 22 September 2021, SSPDF soldiers deserted the SSPDF camp at Nasir and were held by the SPLM/A-IO in Ulang.

They all claimed to have been forcibly recruited by the SSPDF in Lakes State.

The deserters remained | in Ulang under the SPLMW/A-IO until the end of January 2022 when they were reportedly sent back home by the SPLM/A-IO authorities.

| 2. Allegations | a. In June 2022, CTSAMVM MVT Yambio received an allegation on recruitment and training of youth in Tambura by Captain Philips Khamis an officer in charge of South Sudan National Security Services (NSS).

It was alleged that one Captain Philips Khamis started recruitment of the youth in Tambura to increase the number of NSS personnel and to replace those that were lost through desertion.

It was further alleged that NSS lost a significant number of personnel through desertion thus the reason for Captain Philips Khamis to | decide to start recruiting and training the youths in Tambura in order to mitigate the personnel gaps.

Training of these new recruits was allegedly being conducted in the vicinity of Tambura Hospital along Sue road and the area close to the Paramount Chief's residence.

b. Consequently, the Tambura County Local Authorities reportedly asked Captain Philips to provide documentation from the Revitalised Transitional Government of National Unity (RTGoNU) authorizing such recruitment and training activities in the area.

Aim

:

The aim of this report is to present the findings of CTSAMVM investigations into allegations of recruitment and training by the National Security Service in Tambura County. 4.

Findings:

| 5.

The political and security situation in Tambura County has not normalised | because of the disagreements between the State and County leadership.

The | Deputy Governor instructed people to keep staying in the IDP camps because | fighting is still expected while the County Commissioner requested them to go back to their homes.

These contradictions have kept civilians in a state of fear and uncertainty.

| 6.

There is ongoing NSS training currently being conducted in different locations | such as Mamenze across the river, around km south of Tambura and areas close to the Airfield where the NSS Barracks is located. 7.

The Training was authorized by the State Government authorities, the NSS HQ and County authorities.

Captain Phillips Khamis (NSS) started the recruitment drive around 16 May 2022 in Tambura.

| 8.

Senior State officials alluded that the program was supposedly to train a cadre of youth to replace deserters from their operations in Tambura.

However, there were differing reports on the training site, with some saying that it is in Matakurungu near the home of the Tambura Paramount Chief while others said that it is near Tambura Hospital.

There were also differing reports of the composition of the trainees, with some alleging that they were all Zande whilst others claim that they were a mix of Balanda, Zande, Dinka and Jur. 9.

The training includes the former NSS agents who have been working with the Service for years to be nominated to join NSS and new recruits to increase the | numbers of personnel.

| 10.

Captain Philips Khamis told CTSAMVM that the training includes the NSS soldiers who defected or deserted to other areas during the conflicts, indiscipline soldiers and active soldiers to raise their capabilities and readiness. 11.

CTSAMVM confirmed from relatives of the recruits on training that there are some underage children who were recruited and joined training. 12.

There are child soldiers who had joined Maj Gen James Nando during the Tambura conflict but did not go through the screening process.

They have uniforms obtained from Maridi TC which resemble NSS uniform.

These child | | | soldiers donning NSS looking uniforms have become a source of threat inside the IDP Camps. 13.

One of the Paramount Chiefs confirmed to have attended a meeting in March 2022 together with other local chiefs and headmen from both sides of Azande and Balanda, chaired by the County Commissioner.

The commissioner informed them about the new recruitment for those who were willing to join NSS basing on | the request by NSS.

| Assessment | 14.

The security situation in Tambura has improved and the people move freely.

Very few people are still in Hayat IDP Camp especially those who came from remote | areas. 15.

There are under age children among the recruits on training as confirmed by | some of the parents.

CTSAMVM did not confirm whether there was forced recruitment of these children. 16.

The security situation in WES including Tambura is politically motivated.

The people of Tambura especially those in IDP camps are confused by the contradicting instructions given to them by the State and County leaders | belonging to different Party Signatories.

| 17.

There was deliberate effort by Captain Philips Khamis to interrupt and deny | CTSAMVM access to information claiming that the MVT had undermined his | authority by being in his AOR without his knowledge.

However, this was not true | because all the necessary communications and arrangements had been earlier | done by the National Monitors.

This was an unfortunate incident deliberately | intended to deny information to CTSAMVM in the fulfilment of its mandate.

| Recommendations | | 18.

It is strongly recommended that NSS de-registers the children on training and have them return to their parents. 19.

There is need for dialogue amongst the leaders and communities of Tambura to avoid the repeat of June-August 2021 conflict. 20.

Need for reconciliation between Governor of WES and his Deputy to let the State Government work in harmony. 21.
