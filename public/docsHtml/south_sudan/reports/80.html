Following the receipt of reports from the Monitoring and Verification Team (MVT) based in the UNMISS camp near Bentiu that described violations of the Cessation of Hostilities Agreement (COHA) in Rubkona County, Unity State, the JTC initiated formal investigation and verification processes. •

Reports indicate that hostilities between SPLM/A-IO and Government Forces took place in the area of Kilo 30, Rubkona and Bentiu starting on 27 October and finishing on 29 October.

•

On 18-20 October there had been unconfirmed reports of minor hostilities between the SPLM/A-IO and Government Forces in Unity State, and in the following week there were rumours of an impending SPLM/A-IO attack.

Hostilities started early in the morning of 27 October and continued until a relative calm had returned by the morning of 30 October. •

Reports were received by the MVT of civilians being deliberately killed in Bentiu during the fighting.

These allegations are the subject of an ongoing investigation and if proved will be the subject of a separate violation report. •

As aresult of investigations, and the weight of evidence collated it is the opinion of the JTC that SPLM/A-IO Forces violated the following articles of the Cessation of Hostilities Agreement (COHA), 23 January 2014 in Unity State, over the period 27 to 29 October 2014:

2.0 Investigation / Verification Methodology 241 Employing Monitoring and Verification Mechanism (MVM) Standard Operating Procedures, the MVT based in the UNMISS Camps near Bentiu initiated the investigation / verification sequence with a series of reports beginning with a Flash Report at 12.0 Hrs on 27 October.

Subsequently, the JTC formally issued Investigation and Verification Orders to the MVT.

2.0 The MVT conducted investigations in accordance with the orders, in order to augment the information already provided and corroborate reports received from other sources.

The result of investigations were a series of reports ending with a summary submitted on November and then a meeting report submitted on November.

By this time the MVT had been able to patrol in the town, meet both military and civil leaders from the Government and have some limited telephone conversations with the SPLM/A-IO military leadership.
